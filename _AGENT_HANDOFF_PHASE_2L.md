## Phase 2K: Murray Settlement Research Page

**New file:** `Topics/Murray Settlement.md`

Tom Copley's thesis: the Murray/Irish Settlement in Lewis County, WV was not a random cluster of individual immigrants but a **coordinated community transplant** — a Roscommon neighborhood that organized itself to relocate collectively and reconstitute its social world in America.

### Key Content

**Documented settlement families (Section 3):**
- Copleys: Michael Sr. + Patrick on *Powhatan* Aug 20, 1838; 1843 Hoffman deed
- Dolans: Elizabeth Mullooly Dolan (b. 1829, County Roscommon) — confirmed Roscommon origin
- Murrays: Eponymous but no individual yet profiled — central research target
- Others (suspected): Mulrooneys, Mahons, Hannons, Reynolds

**Central hypothesis (Section 4):** Murray family was the anchor — arrived first, wrote home to Kilgefin community, enabled chain migration. The settlement name implies their primacy.

**Ann Munday / Murray question (Section 5 — RQ-M5):** Tom Copley notes "Munday" and "Murray" are phonetically similar. If Ann's surname is a transcription of "Murray," it would place Michael Copley Sr. **inside** the Murray family network through marriage — not merely as a neighbor. **This is the single most leveraged open question in the Murray Settlement investigation.**

**Research strategy (Section 7):** 5 tiers, 16 specific research actions including:
- Lewis County deed records (Weston Courthouse) — who bought land before 1843?
- 1840 US Census FAN-club sweep near Copley parcels
- Catholic parish records (St. Michael's Church / Diocese of Wheeling-Charleston)
- Full *Powhatan* and *Kutusoff* passenger manifests
- Griffith's Valuation Murray/Dolan/Mullooly searches in Roscommon
- Kilgefin parish registers; LDS microfilm 989747
- Lewis County local histories (History of Lewis County WV, 1881)

**Formal research questions (Section 8):** RQ-M1 through RQ-M8 with priority ratings.

**Also updated:** `Topics/Irish Immigration to West Virginia.md` now prominently links to Murray Settlement and expands acquisition strategies. `People/Ann Copley.md` now flags the Murray hypothesis in Research Gap #1.
