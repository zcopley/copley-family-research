The Copleys of Ballymoe and Ballinasloe and the Copleys of Kilgefin (often recorded under Kilkeevin or the Tarmon/Castlerea areas) appear to be closely related branches of the same family settled in County Roscommon, likely sharing a common ancestor in the late 18th century.

The Kilgefin and Kilkeevin Branch (Castlerea/Tarmon)

Records for the Copleys in the Kilgefin and Kilkeevin areas of Roscommon show a well-documented lineage: [1 (https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/163067857)]

Thomas Copley (b. ~1769 – d. 1841) is a central figure in this branch. He lived in Kilkeevin Parish(Castlerea) and married Jane Timms in 1804. [1 (https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/163067857)]

His children included Richard (b. 1807), Robert (b. 1810), and Patrick (b. 1814), with descendants later residing in the townland of Tarmon. [1 (https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/163067857)]

Michael Copley (b. 1813), the son of John and Sarah Copley, is specifically recorded as being from Kilgefin. [1 (https://ancestors.familysearch.org/en/L4ZS-B6W/michael-copley-1813-1897)]

The Ballymoe and Ballinasloe Connection

The relationship between these groups is characterized by their shared geographic and religious background in a relatively small area:

Geographic Proximity: Ballymoe sits on the border of Roscommon and Galway, very near Castlerea (Kilkeevin). Historical records often mention families in this area moving between the Ballymoe Barony(Galway) and the Roscommon Barony (Roscommon) due to overlapping parish boundaries. [1 (https://www.facebook.com/groups/RoscommonIrelandGenealogy/posts/27108029682147707/), 2 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ballymoe), 3 (https://www.genuki.org.uk/big/irl/ROS/parishes), 4 (https://wikishire.co.uk/wiki/County_Roscommon)]

Estate Influence: While some Copley lines in Ireland (like the famous American painter John Singleton Copley) had roots in Limerick or Cork, the Roscommon branches were firmly established as local farmers and tradesmen by the 1800s. [1 (https://www.facebook.com/groups/RoscommonIrelandGenealogy/posts/1911225362254820/), 2 (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mary_Singleton_Copley_Pelham), 3 (https://landedestates.ie/estate/3381), 4 (https://www.findagrave.com/memorial/163067857)]

Religious Diversity: While many Copley families in these parishes were Protestant (associated with the Church of Ireland (https://www.churchofireland.org/)), some lines converted or were listed in Roman Catholic records by the mid-19th century. [1 (https://www.johngrenham.com/findasurname.php?surname=Copley), 2 (https://www.johngrenham.com/c_parish/c_parish.php?county=Roscommon&surname=Copley)]

The Ballymoe & Ballinasloe Link

Ballymoe: The townland of Tarmon, where most Copleys were based, is located in Kilkeevin Parish but sits directly on the border of the Barony of Ballymoe. In historical records, these families are frequently referred to as the “Copleys of Ballymoe” because the village itself spans the border between Roscommon and Galway. [1 (https://catalogue.nli.ie/Record/vtls000302436)]

Ballinasloe: There are fewer rural Copley holdings in the immediate Ballinasloe area during the 1850s, suggesting that members of the Tarmon/Ballymoe branch migrated there later in the century for trade or urban labor. [1 (http://www.ballinasloe.org/articles/genealogy.php), 2 (https://hooperandboylandfamilies.wordpress.com/the-villa-branch/henry-family-from-ballinasloe/)]

Geographic Origins (Kilgefin)

While the primary 1850s cluster is in Tarmon (Kilkeevin), the Kilgefin branch lived roughly 15 miles to the southeast. At the time of Griffith’s Valuation, fewer Copleys remained as primary leaseholders in Kilgefincompared to the earlier 1800s, suggesting a gradual migration toward the Castlerea/Ballymoe cluster or abroad. [1 (http://www.irish-geneaography.com/griffiths-valuation-1847-1864.html), 2 (https://www.failteromhat.com/griffiths/roscommon/kilgefin.htm)]
