# Gatton and Leigh Place Notes

This page captures Tom Copley's family-memory notes about the Gatton / Leigh Place / London Copley branch. Parts of the note now have published corroboration, but the full estate sequence still needs direct record work.

## Tom's Note

- The Gatton land stayed in the family until another son of William married a Weston daughter.
- That son died soon after, leaving the estate to his wife.
- From that point on, the Westons owned the property.
- The family manor was called **Leigh Place**, near modern Gatwick Airport.
- The London Copleys were wealthy until recusancy troubles with Elizabeth's government forced some of them to flee.
- Several generations of London Copleys were members of the **Mercer's Guild**.

## Corroborating Leads

- Richard Copley Christie's 1897 edition of Sir Thomas Copley's letters is likely the key published source for the Gatton recusant correspondence. See [[References/Sir Thomas Copley Letters Notes|Sir Thomas Copley Letters Notes]].
- *Annals of an Old Manor-House: Sutton Place, Guildford* supports the Weston-transfer thread, but gives the mechanism differently: Mary Copley, a great-granddaughter of Sir Thomas, brought the Gatton estates to the Westons when she married John Weston in 1637.
- Historic England confirms Leigh Place as a Grade II* listed house at Leigh, Surrey, and says it was remodelled and extended in the seventeenth century by Margaret Copeley.
- A National Archives Discovery record for C 131/99/8 identifies Roger Copley as a citizen and mercer of London in a Southwark property context, supporting Tom's broader Mercers connection.

## Research Use

- Adds likely context for the London/Gatton branch of the family.
- Suggests a second property thread beyond the Mersham/Gatton material in Dorsey.
- The Weston transfer and Leigh Place connection now have useful published support.
- The exact "another son of William married a Weston daughter" wording should remain tentative because accessible published sources currently point instead to Mary Copley's marriage to John Weston.
- The Mercers connection has at least one strong lead through Roger Copley, citizen and mercer of London, but needs fuller extraction from Mercers' Company records.
- Tom's relationship correction places [[People/William Copley (Woolbedding)|William Copley of Woolbedding]] as the great-uncle of Sir Thomas Copley of Gatton. See the Surrey / London / Gatton branch diagram in [[Topics/Bredon Descent|Bredon Descent]].
