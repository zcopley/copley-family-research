# Gatton and Leigh Place Notes

This page captures Tom Copley's family-memory notes about the Gatton / Leigh Place / London Copley branch. It is useful context, but it is still family-research material rather than a verified estate history.

## Tom's Note

- The Gatton land stayed in the family until another son of William married a Weston daughter.
- That son died soon after, leaving the estate to his wife.
- From that point on, the Westons owned the property.
- The family manor was called **Leigh Place**, near modern Gatwick Airport.
- The London Copleys were wealthy until recusancy troubles with Elizabeth's government forced some of them to flee.
- Several generations of London Copleys were members of the **Mercer's Guild**.

## Research Use

- Adds likely context for the London/Gatton branch of the family.
- Suggests a second property thread beyond the Mersham/Gatton material in Dorsey.
- Needs corroboration before being treated as established genealogy or estate history.
