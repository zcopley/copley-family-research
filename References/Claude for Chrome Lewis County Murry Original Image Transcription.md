# Claude for Chrome Lewis County Murry Original Image Transcription

This note preserves Zach Copley's Claude for Chrome original-image transcription pass for the key Lewis County Murry / Murray vital records.

**Main result:** The original Patrick Murry death-register image confirms that Patrick's parents were written as **Luke & Margaret Murry**. That makes the father-name evidence primary-image supported, not merely a FamilySearch abstraction. The known 1854 Luke Murray remains too close in age to Patrick to be his father, so the likely next research target is an **older Luke Murry and Margaret Murry** couple.

## Kinship Interpretation

The original death image confirms that Patrick's parents were recorded as Luke and Margaret Murry. This no longer rests on a FamilySearch indexing inference.

The record does **not** identify which Luke. Because Patrick was born about 1823 and the known Luke Murray who married Anne Loghan is generally treated as born about 1822, the 1854 Luke is almost certainly too young to be Patrick's father. The evidence now points most strongly to a separate older Luke Murry, likely born circa 1790-1800 in Ireland, married to Margaret Murry, as Patrick's father.

That older Luke / Margaret couple has not yet been directly found in the records checked here.

## Next Research Target

The next high-value task is no longer image transcription of these four records. It is to search for the older **Luke Murry + Margaret** family behind Patrick:

- death, probate, cemetery, land, and tax records in Lewis County / Gilmer County / surrounding West Virginia counties;
- St. Michael's Catholic Church sacramental registers;
- earlier census or immigration records under Murry / Murray / Murrey variants;
- records for Patrick's widow Mary S. Murry / Mary Donahoe and their children that may preserve grandparents or kin.
