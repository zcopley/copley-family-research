# Tom Copley May 2026 Research Corrections

This note preserves two May 2026 corrections from Tom Copley that change the handling of earlier AI-assisted research leads.

## Calendar of State Papers / Arigna Claim

Tom reports that the AI citation to **_Calendar of State Papers, Ireland, 1633-1647_, p. 301** as evidence for Captain John Copley and Arigna is fabricated.

He checked the Internet Archive copy of the volume and found no mention of **Copley** or alternative spellings in the entire volume. This closes the earlier task that treated the citation as possibly real.

**Current handling:** The alleged Calendar of State Papers reference should be treated as a fabricated AI citation and should not be repeated as an open lead. The broader 1641 Arigna-destruction story was already contradicted by chronology and by the absence of a Captain John Copley entry in the 1641 Depositions.

## Ann Munday / Murray Hypothesis

Tom now thinks the Munday-to-Murray connection is probably a false lead. His current view is that Ann was probably **Munday only**, and that there is no proof she was a Murray.

The prior indirect evidence remains useful context:

- no Munday entries found in Kinawley / Fermanagh Griffith's Valuation;
- Murray entries found in Kinawley;
- no independent Munday household found in the Lewis County census checks.

But those are absence / proximity clues, not a direct identity record. They are not enough to override the recorded Munday family tradition.

**Current handling:** Treat Ann's recorded surname **Munday** as the preferred working surname unless a direct record proves otherwise. Keep **Murray** as a tested but unproven and now downgraded hypothesis, not as the working conclusion.
