# Claude for Chrome Lewis County Murry Continuity Search

This note preserves Zach Copley's May 14, 2026 Claude for Chrome continuity search for **Luke Murry / Murray** and the separate adult **Patrick Murry / Murray** after their appearance in the 1860 Lewis County, Virginia census.

**Evidence caution:** This is a browser-based FamilySearch research handoff. It includes strong identity matches through census, marriage, death, and FamilySearch tree source-link evidence, but original image capture should still be pursued for the key marriage and death records before turning the kinship conclusions into proof statements.

## Luke Murry / Murray Continuity

Claude found a confirmed post-1860 identity path for Luke:

| Date | Record | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1854 | Lewis County marriage lead | Luke Murray married **Anne Loghan** in Lewis County, Virginia / West Virginia, according to FamilySearch tree source links. |
| 1870 | North Providence, Providence, Rhode Island census, ARK `MFF4-PXK` | Luke Murray with wife Anne and the same anchor children: Patrick, John, and Mary, all born Virginia. Additional Virginia-born children and Rhode Island-born younger children show the family moved after 1866 and before March 1867. |
| 1880 | Providence, Rhode Island census, ARK `M4SN-98J` | Luke Murray, age 57, Ireland, day laborer, with wife Ann and children; Patrick, John, and Mary remain consistent identity anchors. |
| 1899 | Providence, Rhode Island death record, ARK `F8ZV-9T5` | Luke Murray died 1 November 1899; spouse Ann Murray; parents indexed as Luke and Mary Murray. |

**Conclusion:** Luke did not disappear from records and did not remain in Lewis County. He moved from Lewis County to North Providence / Providence, Rhode Island between late 1866 and March 1867.

## Adult Patrick Murry / Murray Continuity

Claude found a strong continuity path for the separate adult Patrick from the 1860 household:

| Date | Record | Result |
|---|---|---|
| 1862 | Lewis County marriage lead | Patrick Murray married **Mary Donahoe / Donahue** on 27 December 1862 in Lewis County, West Virginia, according to the handoff. |
| 1870 | Sheridan, Lewis County, West Virginia census, ARK `MZ4L-F8K` | Patrick Murray, age 47, Ireland, with wife Mary, children Mary Ann, Martin, Ellen, Margaret, and infant Luke. The FamilySearch event place is indexed as Sheridan Magisterial District, Lincoln County, but Claude reports the original census text reads **Sheridan, Lewis, West Virginia**. |
| 1871 | West Virginia death record, ARK `NMD7-M2L` | Patrick Murry died 22 August 1871 at Sand Fork of Kent, Lewis County, West Virginia; age 48; married; farmer; spouse Mary S. Murry; parents Luke Murry and Margaret Murry. |

**Conclusion:** Adult Patrick did not follow Luke to Rhode Island. He remained in Lewis County, married Mary Donahoe / Donahue, appeared in the 1870 Sheridan / Lewis census, and died in Lewis County in 1871.

## Kinship Implication

The 1871 death record naming Patrick's father as **Luke Murry** is important because Luke Murry's own FamilySearch tree also gives a father named Luke Murray. That is consistent with the working hypothesis that 1860 household head Luke Murry and adult Patrick Murry were brothers.

This remains a kinship hypothesis until the underlying marriage, death, church, probate, or family records are imaged and evaluated directly.

## Interpretation for RQ-M1

This continuity search changes the census interpretation:

- The earlier statement that no Ireland-born Murray / Murry household appeared in 1870 Lewis County must be narrowed. Luke was no longer in Lewis County by 1870, but adult Patrick appears to have remained there in **Sheridan, Lewis County**, under a record that FamilySearch partially misindexed as Lincoln County.
- The 1860 Murry presence does not yet prove a pre-1843 anchor family. Luke and adult Patrick first appear in the current workflow in 1860 and likely arrived in Lewis County by the mid-1850s.
- The Murry / Murray line now has a clearer local continuity path through adult Patrick, Sand Fork of Kent, and the Mary Donahoe / Donahue marriage.
- Anne Loghan / Logan becomes a new local network lead because Owen Loghan / Logan in the 1860 household likely belonged to her family.
