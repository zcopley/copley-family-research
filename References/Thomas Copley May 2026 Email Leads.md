## Evidence Caution

These emails are useful as a map of research leads, not as proof. The body of the messages is primarily AI-generated analysis and includes claims that must be checked against original records before being repeated as fact.

For the current public history:

- Do **not** state that John Copley and Sarah Dolan are proven parents of Michael Copley Sr.
- Do **not** state that a specific Sarah Dolan of Kilgefin has been identified.
- Do **not** rely on the AI's Griffith's Valuation, Tithe Applotment, parish-start-date, or Strokestown archive assertions without checking the underlying source.
- Do treat Tom's email as a useful signpost toward family-held records, probate files, and collateral descendants.

## Highest-Value Lead

The most actionable item is in Tom's 8 May 2026 email to Steve and Zach. Tom wrote that the AI, after follow-up questioning, suggested that **someone in Bridget Copley's line might hold a Copley family Bible showing John and Sarah as Michael's parents**, and that this Bible may once have been cited in **Lewis County court clerk probate documents**.

This is unverified, but it matters because it points to a testable proof path:

- Identify [[People/Bridget Copley Reynolds|Bridget Copley Reynolds]] descendants, if she emigrated and had descendants.
- Search Lewis County probate, estate, chancery, heirship, and oil-lease litigation records after [[People/Michael Copley Sr|Michael Copley Sr.]] died in January 1897.
- Look specifically for affidavits, Bible extracts, family-record exhibits, heirship schedules, or attorney filings naming Michael's parents.
- Search the 1900 Copley Oil Pool / "Michael Copley Heirs" title and lease trail for documents that required proof of heirship.

If found, a Bible extract or probate exhibit would be much stronger evidence than naming-pattern arguments.

## Parentage Hypothesis: John Copley and Sarah Dolan

The emails repeatedly ask whether **John Copley** and **Sarah Dolan** of Kilgefin / Roscommon can be proved as Michael's parents. The AI responses cite naming patterns, sibling migration, land clusters, and later family memory.

Current handling:

- Treat naming patterns as weak supporting context only.
