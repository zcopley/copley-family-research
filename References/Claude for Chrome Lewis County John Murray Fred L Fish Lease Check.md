# Claude for Chrome Lewis County John Murray Fred L Fish Lease Check

This note captures the Claude for Chrome follow-up on the long-carried FamilySearch index lead for **John Murray -> Fred L. Fish**. Its main value is corrective: the FamilySearch computer index date of **1826** is wrong.

## Main Result

- The indexed entry previously treated in this project as an **1826** John Murray deed is actually a **1899 oil and gas lease**.
- The FamilySearch structured "Event Date: 1826" is a computer-indexing error.
- The deed text is fully located in **DGS 008293302**, image **52**, page **39**, and is dated **October 7, 1899**.
- Because the instrument is late-19th-century and not pre-1843, it has **no bearing** on the RQ-M1 anchor-family question.

## Lease Abstract

- **Grantor:** John Murray
- **Grantee:** Fred L. Fish
- **Date written / acknowledged:** October 7, 1899
- **Type:** pre-printed oil and gas lease
- **Acreage:** about **132 acres**
- **Consideration:** **$32.50**, plus **$7.00 per year** if gas only is found
- **Royalty:** grantor reserved **1/8 of all oil produced**
- **County:** Lewis County, West Virginia
- **Neighbors:** east **Geo. White & alts**; south **Thomas White**; west **Mrs. [Stidger?/Ridget?] Cummings & alts**; north neighbor partly illegible

## Interpretation for RQ-M1

- This does **not** place John Murray in Lewis County before 1843.
- This does **not** identify an early Murray anchor-family tract.
- This does **not** strengthen or weaken the Murray Settlement hypothesis in itself.
- It should be preserved as a **controlled misindex / closed lead**, not as an active pre-1843 deed target.

## Effect on the Murray Workflow

- The supposed **1826 Murray / Fish** lead is now closed.
- The built-in Deed Book C-D **Marwee** path was already retired.
- The remaining direct early Murray deed target is now the **1833 John Murray** entry.
