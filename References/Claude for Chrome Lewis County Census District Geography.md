# Claude for Chrome Lewis County Census District Geography

This note preserves Zach Copley's May 11, 2026 Claude for Chrome district-mapping output for the Lewis County FAN-sweep workstream. It tests how the **1850 District 30**, **1860 blank rural sub-district**, and **1870 Battelle / Willey** census labels relate to Weston, Cove Lick, Camden, Loveberry, and the Murray Settlement geography.

**Evidence caution:** This is a browser-based census-header and map review. It is useful for geographic orientation, but it does not prove exact township boundaries or parcel-level residence.

## Map Evidence

The reviewed map was M. Wood White's 1875 **White's County and District Map of the State of West Virginia**, available through the David Rumsey Map Collection:

<https://www.davidrumsey.com/luna/servlet/detail/RUMSEY~8~1~361995~90129517>

The Lewis County labels visible on the map are:

- **Freemans Creek** in northwestern Lewis County.
- **Hackers Creek** in northeastern Lewis County near Weston.
- **Courthouse** in central Lewis County, containing Weston.
- **Collins Settlement** in southern Lewis County.
- **Skidmore** in eastern Lewis County.

The map does **not** label `Battelle Township` or `Willey District`. The safest conclusion is that the 1870 census township labels and the 1875 magisterial-district map labels do not line up cleanly from this map alone.

## Geographic Interpretation

The district-mapping pass strongly supports a **Weston / courthouse-hinterland** interpretation for the 1850-1870 Irish farming evidence:

- The 1860 Murry / Ryan / Carroll pages use **Weston, Lewis Co. Va.** as the post office.
- The 1870 Battelle image 8 cluster also uses **Weston, West Va.** as the post office.
- The 1850 Coopley page has no post office field, but the District No. 30 / 1860 / 1870 sequence makes it plausible that District No. 30 overlapped the later Weston-centered rural area.

This **does not prove** that the Battelle cluster was in Cove Lick, Camden, or Loveberry. Those place names did not appear in the reviewed census headers or index data. The current evidence points more directly to the rural farming territory around Weston than to the southern place-name cluster.

## New Lead

During the Willey header check, Claude surfaced **Mary Casey**, born about 1830 in Ireland, in Willey district, household 8, line 39, ARK `MZ4L-G5R`. A later follow-up in [[References/Claude for Chrome Lewis County Mary Casey Household]] confirmed that she belonged to a Thomas / Mary Casey household: Thomas and Mary were born in Ireland and their son John was born in Virginia.

## Remaining Gaps

- Exact 1850 District No. 30 boundaries remain unproven.
- The 1860 rural sub-district name is blank in the census; another source would be needed to map it.
- The 1870 Battelle and Willey labels do not appear on the 1875 White map under those names.
- The 1860 assistant marshal surname and 1870 Battelle assistant marshal surname remain partially unread.
- Willey district's exact relationship to Weston, Battelle, Cove Lick, Camden, and Loveberry remains unresolved.
- The adjacent John Byrne and Thomas Carey households still need a narrow birthplace check.
