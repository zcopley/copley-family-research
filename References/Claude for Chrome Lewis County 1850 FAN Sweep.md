# Claude for Chrome Lewis County 1850 FAN Sweep

This note captures the first bounded Claude for Chrome census-neighbor pass for the Murray Settlement question: the **1850 Lewis County, Virginia census**.

## Summary

The 1850 sweep found the **Coopley** household as the main Irish-origin target in Lewis County, but it did **not** find an Irish-born Murray household or a broader Irish Catholic surname cluster in that year.

**Main takeaway:** The 1850 result does not confirm a Murray-centered settlement cluster. It instead suggests:

- the **Coopleys were present early** and established in Lewis County by 1850
- the broader surname cluster around **Dolan, Hanley, Hannon, Reynolds, Mahon, Mullooly, Mulroney, Gillooly**, and related names does **not yet appear** in Lewis County in 1850
- the best next census step is now **1860**, not 1840, because Dolans and other related families appear to enter the Lewis County record later

## Key Positive Findings

### Micheal / Patrick Coopley Household

- **Written surname:** `Coopley`
- **Location:** District 30, Lewis County, Virginia
- **Image:** 209 of 237
- **Household:** Micheal Coopley (36, Ireland, farmer, $1,000), Ann Coopley (26, Ireland), Patrick Coopley (48, Ireland, laborer), with Virginia-born children John, Mary, Catharine, and Ann
- **Significance:** This is the strongest positive 1850 Irish-origin household in the target surname set. It confirms the Copleys were firmly established in Lewis County by 1850, with children already born in Virginia.

### John Murray Household

- **Written surname:** `Murray`
- **Location:** district not confirmed from image header
- **Image:** 37 of 237
- **Household:** John Murray (48, Virginia), wife Jane (42, Virginia), and 10 Virginia-born children
- **Significance:** This appears to be a multi-generation Virginia family, not an immigrant Irish-origin Murray household tied clearly to the settlement question.

### Hudson Murray Household

- **Written surname:** `Murray`
- **Location:** district not confirmed from image header
- **Image:** 195 of 237
- **Household:** Hudson Murray (50, Virginia), Elizabeth Murray (50, Virginia)
- **Significance:** Older Virginia-born couple; not obviously relevant to the immigrant Irish settlement question.

## Cluster Interpretation

- The **Coopley** household in District 30 appears relatively isolated in 1850 rather than embedded in a dense Irish Catholic cluster.
- The immediate neighbors around Micheal / Patrick Coopley were largely Scottish- or Virginia-born families, not the expected Dolan / Hanley / Hannon-type cluster.
- A **Thomas White** household appears nearby, which may matter later because **Thomas White** also appears as a boundary surname in the 1899 John Murray oil-and-gas lease context.
- The **John Murray** household appears to belong to an older Virginia-born Murray family rather than the immigrant Irish-origin line the project is trying to prove.

## RQ-M1 Interpretation

- The 1850 census **weakens and reframes** the Murray Settlement hypothesis in its strongest form.
- It does **not** eliminate the framework, but it suggests the recognizable multi-family Irish Catholic cluster may have formed more clearly in the **1850s**, not before 1850.
- The Coopleys remain the strongest documented early Irish household in Lewis County in 1850.
- The next best test is the **1860 Lewis County census**, where Dolans and other associated surnames begin to appear.
