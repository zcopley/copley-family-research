# Claude for Chrome Lewis County Byrne Carey Household

This note preserves Zach Copley's May 13, 2026 Claude for Chrome follow-up on the **John Byrne** and **Carey / Casey continuation** entries in the 1870 Lewis County, West Virginia census. It resolves the adjacent Byrne / Carey lead from [[References/Claude for Chrome Lewis County Mary Casey Household]].

**Evidence caution:** This is a browser-based FamilySearch extraction using the Names panel, index records, DOM extraction, and image review. It is sufficient for research direction, but the original census images should still be captured and read directly for proof-grade citation, especially for the `Carey` versus `Casey` handwriting issue.

**Follow-up status:** The May 14, 2026 image-capture pass in [[References/Claude for Chrome Lewis County Census Image Capture]] confirms that image 210 is a continuation of the same Casey / Carey household across the page break. The exact written surname remains ambiguous, but the household boundary is no longer treated as a separate Carey family.

## Carey / Casey Continuation

The main correction from this follow-up is structural: the supposed separate 8-person `Carey` household on image 210 appears in the FamilySearch index as a **continuation of Thomas Casey's household 8**, which begins on image 209.

The `Carey` children are not Ireland-born. The birthplace pattern is internally consistent with a family already living in Virginia before West Virginia statehood: older children born in Virginia, younger children born in West Virginia.

The written surname remains unresolved. The index keeps Thomas, Mary, and John as `Casey`, but indexes the children as `Carey`. The image viewer toolbar obscured the first lines of image 210 at useful zoom levels, so the safest reading is that the entries are either:

- Casey children indexed or written with a variant hand that FamilySearch rendered as `Carey`; or
- Carey-surnamed children living in the same household as Thomas and Mary Casey, possibly through a blended-family relationship.

Do not treat `Carey` as a separate household until the original image is checked at higher resolution.

## Interpretation for RQ-M1

This follow-up modestly strengthens the **Willey Irish presence** by confirming John and Ellen Byrne as a second Ireland-born couple on the same census page as Thomas and Mary Casey.

It also corrects the earlier workflow assumption: the `Carey` entries on image 210 do **not** clearly represent an independent Irish-origin household. They appear to continue the Thomas / Mary Casey household across the page break. At minimum, those children are Irish-American by association with Ireland-born household adults; they are not themselves Ireland-born.

The result keeps Willey as a smaller, scattered Irish presence rather than a dense same-page cluster like Battelle image 8. The strongest 1870 rural Irish clustering still appears in Battelle, while Willey now has confirmed Hanahan / Hanrahan, Cummings, Casey, and Byrne evidence across separate pages.
