# Father Thomas Copley - Dorsey Notes

Source:
- Katherine Fitzsimmons Dorsey, *The Life of Father Thomas Copley: A Founder of Maryland* (Georgetown, 1885)
- Available in text form at [Calameo](https://www.calameo.com/books/0001070449338eac461b8)

## Why It Matters

This book is a secondary synthesis, but it contains several useful clues about the Copley family's property and recusant history:

- Thomas Copley obtained Mersham Park in 1568 and settled it on his wife and children.
- Dorsey says the family had already made settlements that prevented forfeiture when his lands were being solicited.
- Mrs. Copley came from Gatton to handle inquiries into the lands and goods of people who had gone overseas without license.
- Dorsey frames the family as wealthy English gentry with Gatton, Leigh Grange, Colley, Southwark, and Mersham Park in their orbit.

## Short Excerpts

> "He at once settled it on his wife and children."

> "he having made settlements on his family which prevented their forfeiture"

> "Mrs. Copley comes from Gatton to look into this"

## Research Use

- Supports the broader argument that the Copley family retained legal property structures even during recusant pressure and exile.
- Strengthens the Gatton/Mersham source trail, but does not replace a direct legal record.
- Useful as a bridge between family tradition, DNB, and the Surrey/VCH material.
