The Murrays of Kilgefin were part of the ancient Siol Muireadhaigh (https://www.irishamerica.com/2018/09/the-murray-clan/) (Silmurray) family, a native Irish sept that held power in County Roscommon since the 700s. By the 19th century, they were established as a prominent Catholic family in the Parish of Kilgefin (https://www.johngrenham.com/c_parish/c_parish.php?county=Roscommon&surname=Murray) (Ballagh), living as neighbors and kin to the Copleys. Relationship to the Copleys

The connection between the Murrays and Copleys was defined by intermarriage and shared migrationpatterns:

Neighboring Families: Records from the mid-1800s place the Murrays in townlands like Aghalahardand Kilgefin, immediately adjacent to the Copley holdings. [1 (https://www.wikitree.com/wiki/Murray-28250), 2 (https://www.facebook.com/groups/443512741460356/posts/444221524722811/)]

In the Tithe Applotment Books (1824-1837) (https://titheapplotmentbooks.nationalarchives.ie/search/tab/results.jsp?surname=&firstname=&county=Roscommon&townland=Aughmuck&parish=Kilgefin&search=Search&sort=&pageSize=&pager.offset=20), the Murray presence was significant:

After this, branches of the family moved into parishes like Kilgefin and Kilmurray to work as farmers. The distribution of wealth from the Copley Oil Pool is a well-documented chapter in West Virginia's history, centered on the landmark discovery of oil on the property of the Michael Copley Heirs in 1900. The Michael Copley Heirs Gusher

On September 13, 1900, the South Penn Oil Company completed the "Michael Copley Heirs No. 1" well on Sand Fork. It was a massive "gusher" that flowed wildly, producing approximately 7,000 to 10,000 barrels of oil per day. Because Michael Copley had passed away in 1897, the mineral rights and subsequent wealth were split among his descendants, primarily his children with Anne Murray/Monday. [, 2 (https://pubs.geoscienceworld.org/aapg/aapgbull/article/11/6/581/544597/The-Copley-Oil-Pool-of-West-Virginia), 3 (https://wvhistoryonview.org/catalog/007933), 4 (https://www.mineralrightsforum.com/t/inherited-78-acres-of-mineral-rights/74404)]

Wealth Distribution & Probate

In West Virginia during this era, mineral rights were typically treated as real property and inherited in equal percentages by each surviving child and their own heirs. [1 (https://www.mineralrightsforum.com/t/inherited-78-acres-of-mineral-rights/74404)]

Equal Division: The wealth from the Copley Pool was distributed among the "Michael Copley Heirs,"which included your ancestor John Copley and his siblings (Patrick, William, Bridget, Catherine, etc.).[1 (https://pubs.geoscienceworld.org/aapg/aapgbull/article/11/6/581/544597/The-Copley-Oil-Pool-of-West-Virginia), 2 (https://www.mineralrightsforum.com/t/inherited-78-acres-of-mineral-rights/74404)]

Estate Records: Detailed probate files and appraisements for the Copley family are held by the Lewis County Clerk (https://www.lewiscountywv.org/county-clerk). These documents list the specific valuation of the estate and the individual shares owed to each child. [1 (https://www.lewiscountywv.org/county-clerk), 2 (https://www.wvrecordsearch.com/west-virginia/lewis-county/deed-search)]

Mineral Rights Legacy: Because mineral rights were often severed from the surface land, some descendants of the original 12 children may still hold small fractional interests in the oil and gas rights today. [1 (https://www.mineralrightsforum.com/t/inherited-78-acres-of-mineral-rights/74404)]
