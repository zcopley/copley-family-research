# Claude for Chrome Lewis County 1860 FAN Sweep

This note preserves Zach Copley's May 8, 2026 Claude for Chrome output for the **1860 Lewis County, Virginia FAN sweep** around the Murray Settlement question.

**Evidence caution:** This is a research handoff from a browser-based FamilySearch session. Treat it as a high-value census navigation and extraction aid until the underlying images are independently captured, read, and cited.

**Follow-up status:** A May 14, 2026 image-capture pass in [[References/Claude for Chrome Lewis County Census Image Capture]] directly checked the key 1860 pages. It confirms the Dolan, Murry, and Weston Loftus / Mc Mahan evidence while clarifying several household boundaries.

**Continuity status:** A later May 14, 2026 follow-up in [[References/Claude for Chrome Lewis County Murry Continuity Search]] traces Luke Murry to Providence, Rhode Island by 1870 and traces the separate adult Patrick Murry to Sheridan / Lewis County in 1870 and death at Sand Fork of Kent, Lewis County, in 1871.

## Major Findings

- **1860 is the first FAN-sweep pass to show a dense Lewis County Irish-born cluster.** The strongest rural cluster is Dolan-centered on pages 116-117 / images 122-123.
- **Ireland-born Luke Murry and adult Patrick Murry are present in Lewis County in 1860.** The image-capture pass confirms that page 114 / image 120 includes Luke Murry, his probable wife Ann, Virginia-born children, and a separate adult Patrick Murry, age 34, born Ireland. The household also includes a 4-year-old Virginia-born child named Patrick, so there are two Patrick Murrys in the same household.
- **The two adult Murry men diverged after 1860.** Luke moved to Rhode Island by 1870. Adult Patrick remained in Lewis County, appeared in Sheridan / Lewis in 1870, and died at Sand Fork of Kent in 1871.
- **The cluster is broader than a simple Murray anchor model.** The strongest evidence points to a rural Irish Catholic farming cluster involving Dolan, McLaughlin, Rogers / Rogan, Burke, Fealey, Buck, Malady, and nearby Murry households.
- **The Coopleys vanish from the 1860 index.** This is a critical gap because Micheal and Patrick Coopley were found in Lewis County in 1850.
- **A separate Weston Irish urban cluster appears.** The image-capture pass confirms the Michael Loftus household includes Ireland-born boarders Miles Barret and James Mc Mahan. It also clarifies that John Loftus is in a separate same-image Lyttle boarding-house cluster with many Ireland-born laborers.

## Interpretation for RQ-M1

The 1860 result **strengthens the Lewis County Irish-settlement framework**, but it reframes it away from a purely Murray-named anchor family and toward a broader Irish Catholic rural cluster. Luke and Patrick Murry matter because they are Ireland-born Murry / Murray candidates in Lewis County, but the densest visible cluster is Dolan-centered and includes several other Irish-born families.

The later continuity search further clarifies that Luke and adult Patrick were not a stable 1870 Lewis County Murray household together. Luke left for Rhode Island, while adult Patrick stayed in Lewis County. That weakens a simple Murray-led anchor reading but gives a better trail for local Murry kinship and locality work.

The most important negative finding is the absence of indexed Copley / Coopley households in 1860. This prevents a direct 1860 census-neighbor link between the 1850 Coopley household and the Dolan / Murry cluster.
