# Claude for Chrome Lewis County Census Image Capture

This note preserves Zach Copley's May 14, 2026 Claude for Chrome image-capture pass for the key 1860 and 1870 Lewis County census pages in the Murray Settlement workflow. It follows the earlier 1850 / 1860 / 1870 FAN sweeps, 1870 image review, district-geography check, Mary Casey check, and Byrne / Carey check.

**Evidence caution:** This is still a browser-based FamilySearch extraction, but it is stronger than the earlier index-only handoffs because it includes direct image reads for many target lines. Some entries remain partially dependent on FamilySearch index records because the image-viewer toolbar blocked top lines or because image quality was faded.

**Continuity status:** A later May 14, 2026 follow-up in [[References/Claude for Chrome Lewis County Murry Continuity Search]] traces Luke Murry to Providence, Rhode Island, and adult Patrick Murry to Sheridan / Lewis County and an 1871 Lewis County death record.

## Interpretation for RQ-M1

This image-capture pass materially strengthens the broader Irish-cluster interpretation. It directly confirms:

- the 1860 Murry household as a real Ireland-born Murry / Murray presence, with both Luke Murry and a separate adult Patrick Murry;
- the 1860 Dolan households as established Ireland-born farming families with Ireland-born older children and Virginia-born younger children;
- a separate 1860 Weston Ireland-born labor / boarding context around Loftus, Barret, Mc Mahan, and other laborers;
- the 1870 Battelle image 8 cluster as a dense, propertied Ireland-born farming page;
- Willey as a smaller, scattered Ireland-born presence, not as dense as Battelle.

The later continuity search changes the Murray-specific conclusion: Luke and adult Patrick Murry no longer simply disappear after 1860. Luke moved to Rhode Island by 1870, while adult Patrick remained in Lewis County, appeared in Sheridan / Lewis in 1870, and died at Sand Fork of Kent in 1871. This gives local Murry continuity through Patrick, but it still does not prove a pre-1843 Murray anchor family.
