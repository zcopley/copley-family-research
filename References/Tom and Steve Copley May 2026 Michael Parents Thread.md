## Evidence Caution

This thread is family correspondence and should be used mainly to sharpen the research plan. It does **not** prove that Michael's parents were John Copley and Sarah Dolan.

The referenced Ancestry / U.S. death-index clue is especially tentative because:

- the image or record extract referenced as `IMG_0212.png` was not included in the forwarded archive;
- Tom reported that the index named Michael's parents as John and Sarah Copley but also had Michael's spouse wrong;
- Steve thought the apparent John / Sarah / Bridget data could have been confused with Michael and Ann's children, including daughters Bridget and Sarah;
- no original death record, informant statement, Bible page, probate exhibit, or will image has been captured in this thread.

## Useful Takeaways

### Parentage hypothesis remains possible but unproven

Tom and Steve both treated **John Copley and Sarah Dolan** as possible parents, but neither presented direct evidence. Steve wrote that John and Sarah were possible and that he knew of no contrary evidence. Tom separately emphasized that the evidence was thin and that the Dolan surname came from Google AI, not from a verified original record.

### Michael's will is probably not the proof source

Both Tom and Steve had looked at Michael's will or knew of it as a microfilm source. Steve described it as very blurry and doubted that it mentioned Michael's parents. Tom later wrote that he had recently read the will, that it made no mention of Michael's parents, and that Michael signed with an `X`.

Current handling: still obtain and transcribe the will if possible, but do not treat it as a likely parentage source unless a better image contradicts the family reading.

### The Bridget / Bible lead is now ambiguous

The earlier May 2026 lead preserved an AI-suggested family Bible path through "Bridget Copley's line." This later thread makes the identity of that Bridget less clear.

Tom speculated that **Bridget**, possibly Michael's daughter [[People/Bridget Bitty Copley Gillooly|Bridget "Bitty" Copley Gillooly]], might have been the informant behind the death-index parent names and might have held or known of a Copley Bible. He also wondered whether a Bible could be in the hands of a Gillooly descendant. That conflicts with the current family narrative that Bridget Gillooly had no children, so the lead should be tracked as an ambiguity rather than assigned confidently to one branch.

Current handling:

- Do not assume the Bible lead belongs specifically to [[People/Bridget Copley Reynolds|Bridget Copley Reynolds]].
- Do not assume it belongs specifically to [[People/Bridget Bitty Copley Gillooly|Bridget "Bitty" Copley Gillooly]] either.
- Search both the Reynolds possibility and the Gillooly / collateral-Gillooly possibility, while also checking whether the original AI output simply conflated multiple Bridgets.

### Dolan memory should not be overread

Tom remembered his father introducing the family to Dolans in [[Places/Weston West Virginia|Weston]] and had assumed those Dolans were probably relatives of [[People/Mary Ellen Dolan Copley|Mary Ellen Dolan Copley]]. In the thread, he raised the possibility that they could also connect to an earlier Sarah Dolan in Ireland, but he immediately framed that as uncertain.

Current handling: Weston Dolan family memory supports the importance of the Dolan network, but it does not prove Sarah Dolan or connect the Weston Dolans to Michael's proposed mother.

## Research Actions Added

1. Search for the underlying Ancestry / death-index source that named John and Sarah and inspect the original image, collection title, informant fields, spouse field, and indexing rules.
2. Obtain a better image or courthouse copy of Michael Copley Sr.'s will, primarily to document the estate and signature-by-mark, while expecting that it may not name parents.
3. Treat the family Bible lead as a **Bridget-identity problem**: Reynolds line, Bridget Gillooly, Gillooly collateral relatives, or AI conflation.
4. Keep Sarah Dolan as a parentage hypothesis only until a direct or near-direct record names her.
