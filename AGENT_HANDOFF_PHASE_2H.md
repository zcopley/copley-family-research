## 3) Corrections & New Info from Tom Copley (April 19, 2026)

### 3a. Mary Skelly (not "Shelly")
The *"Copley Name from Castlerea"* 11-page report was compiled by **Mary Skelly**. All prior files had "Mary Shelly" — corrected everywhere.

### 3b. Captain John's Parentage — Now Clarified
**Old text:** "grandson of Thomas Copley Sr."
**Corrected:** Captain John was a **younger son of Thomas Copley Jr.** of Bredon's Norton. Tom says the record is "a little muddled" but the Visitations of Worcestershire make this clear.

Updated in: research page Sections 2b, 5, 14 (Tom's Hypothesis), both generation narrative sections; CLAUDE.md; GEMINI.md; AGENT_HANDOFF_PHASE_2G.md; CHANGELOG.md.

### 3c. 1634 Visitation — 4th Primary Source for Captain John
The **1634 Visitation of Worcestershire** names "Captain John Copley" by name and records him as **"in Italy"**. Tom suggests (per Google AI) he may have been studying Italian iron smelting techniques — consistent with his later Kingswood ironworks venture. This is now the **4th independent primary source** for Captain John (not 3 as previously stated).

Both the **1569 and 1634 Visitations** are available online as PDFs — just Google them. Tom has copies if needed.

Primary sources are now:
1. Visitations of Worcestershire (1634) — names him, "in Italy"
2. *Journal of the House of Commons* (1642) — Parliament petition re: Ireland
3. *Council Book of Youghal* (1651) — "Captain Copley" as Clerk of the Market
4. Dud Dudley, *Mettallum Martis* (1665) — Kingswood ironworks; moved to Ireland 1657

### 3d. William Copley / Bennett Challoner — Origin Corrected
The William Copley who married Bennett Challoner was from **"Wool Bedding"** in Co. Sussex (likely **Woolbedding** or Beddingfield) — **not** Bredon's Norton. Thomas Copley Sr. (son of William) and Thomas Copley Jr. (grandson) are the confirmed residents of the Bredon's Norton manor house.

### 3e. Raleigh-Copley Connection — Upgraded to Plausible
Tom considers the Raleigh connection "more or less confirmed." It runs through the **Hoo family**: a Copley married a Hoo daughter; so did a Boleyn and a Carew (relative of Raleigh's wife). This connects the Copleys to Anne Boleyn and thus to Queen Elizabeth I. Upgraded from ❓ SPECULATIVE to ⚠️ PLAUSIBLE in the research page.

### 3f. The Southwell Cousinage — New Section Added
Tom describes a broad "cousinage" of Copleys, Raleigh, Boleyns, and Southwells — all distantly related to Queen Elizabeth I — who used these connections to obtain royal favor and Irish appointments. Key documented facts (Tom's assessment: "well documented"):

- **Bridget Copley** married **Sir Richard Southwell**
- Bridget served as Queen Elizabeth's **Latin tutor** for several decades — considered a "very learned lady"
- Their son was **Robert Southwell** (c. 1561–1595): Jesuit priest, executed Catholic martyr, poet — author of *"The Burning Babe"* written in the Tower of London before his execution
- Later Southwells held **military and naval offices in Ireland**
- One Southwell is credited with **introducing the potato to Ireland** (having sailed with Raleigh)

Added to Section 2a of the research page as a new ⚠️ [PLAUSIBLE] paragraph.

### 3g. Christopher Copley / Mary Jones — Corrected
**Old characterization:** "bastard son of Christopher Copley and Mary Jones"
**Tom's correction (April 2026):** Christopher and Mary Jones were **actually married** — this was Christopher's **second marriage** (his first wife died in England, where he had several children). Mary Jones outlived Christopher. Their marriage produced **only one documented child: a daughter, Francis**, who married **Thomas Coote** from a prominent Anglo-Irish family. There is no record of any son from this union. If a son existed who could be our ancestor, he must have been by another woman.

Updated in: research page Section 9, Section 14 (Steve's Hypothesis and comparison table); CLAUDE.md; GEMINI.md; AGENT_HANDOFF_PHASE_2G.md.

### 3h. Alternate Name Spellings Added
When said with a heavy Irish brogue, the Copley name may appear as:
- **"Coplet"** — Tom has heard this variant
- **"Coplay"** — Tom has heard this variant

These join the existing known variants: "Copley" (Protestant branch), "Copely" (Catholic branch). Added to Section 13 of the research page and to CLAUDE.md.
