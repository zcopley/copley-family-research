# Wyndmoor, Pennsylvania

## Historical Context (1900s)
For this family, Wyndmoor matters as a federal-science and wartime home-front setting rather than as an immigrant-origin place. Its significance comes from proximity to the USDA's Eastern Regional Research Laboratory and to the wider Philadelphia research corridor.

- **Institutional context:** Eastern Regional Research Laboratory (ERRL), USDA.
- **Era context:** World War II home-front research, suburban family life, and early scientific-professional mobility.
- **Mobility role:** bridge between the Lewis County / Weston generation and the postwar California branch.

## Copley Family Connection
- [[Michael Joseph Copley]] moved to Wyndmoor in `1939` after accepting leadership of the **Physical Chemistry Division** at the USDA Eastern Regional Research Laboratory.
- [[Stephen Michael Copley]]'s appendix sketch treats Wyndmoor as the setting of his childhood from roughly `1939-1947`, including World War II rationing, gardening, private-school education, and visits with the Sardo branch.
- [[Marion Elizabeth Partlow]]'s profile also places the family in Wyndmoor during Michael Joseph's ERRL years before the `1947` move to California.
