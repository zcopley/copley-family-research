# Ellicott City, Maryland

## Geographic Information
- 🗺️ **Google Maps:** [View on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Ellicott+City+Maryland)
- **Approx. coordinates:** 39.2673, -76.7983
- **Current administration:** Ellicott City, Howard County, Maryland.
- **Historical names/boundaries:** a long-standing mill and rail-linked town west of Baltimore.

## Historical Context (1900s)
Within this project, Ellicott City appears as an educational waypoint connected to rail access out of West Virginia and into Catholic schooling in Maryland.

- **Education context:** Rock Hill College boarding-school lead.
- **Transportation context:** rail access from the Weston region into the Baltimore / Potomac corridor.
- **Interpretive role:** illustrates how one branch moved from rural Lewis County into broader Catholic and academic networks.

## Copley Family Connection
- The appendix sketch for [[Michael Joseph Copley]] says he likely attended **Rock Hill College** in Ellicott City.
- That same sketch reports that a narrow-gauge line linked Weston to the B&O main line and that Ellicott City had a station serving the college route.
- Even without full school-record confirmation yet, this gives a concrete geographic step between Michael Joseph's Weston childhood and his later studies at [[Places/Baltimore Maryland|Baltimore]], Catholic University, and the University of Illinois.
