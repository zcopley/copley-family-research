# Weston, West Virginia

## Geographic Information
- 🗺️ **Google Maps:** [View on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Weston+West+Virginia)
- **Coordinates:** 39.0418, -80.4673
- **Current administration:** City of Weston, Lewis County seat, West Virginia.
- **Historical names/boundaries:** records may appear under Lewis County, Virginia (pre-1863) and later West Virginia.

## Historical Context (1800s-1900s)
As county seat, Weston concentrated legal, church, and market functions for surrounding farm communities. This made it a key records hub for marriage, probate, and land evidence.

- **Economy:** county-seat services + surrounding agricultural production.
- **Administrative role:** deed/probate/marriage archive center.
- **Church context:** Catholic records in/near Weston are significant to Copley research.
- **Town-country relationship:** the family-history narrative treats Weston as the nearby growing city that anchored services and institutions for the more isolated Copley farm country.

## Local Family Geography
- The appendix sketch for [[People/Michael Joseph Copley]] says his aunt [[People/Sarah Copley|Sarah Copley]] lived at `108 Cottage Ave., Weston`, across the West Fork River from the West Allegheny Lunatic Asylum.
- The same sketch places Michael Joseph and [[People/John Copley|John Copley]] at `202 W. 2nd St., Weston` in the 1920 census, showing the family's shift from farm-centered geography into town addresses.
- These address details come from a family-history appendix, not yet from independent city-directory or deed confirmation, but they are useful orientation points for future Weston research.
