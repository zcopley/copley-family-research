# Loveberry Ridge, West Virginia

## Geographic Information
- 🗺️ **Google Maps:** [View on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Loveberry+Ridge+Weston+West+Virginia)
- **Approx. coordinates:** 39.04, -80.50 *(local ridge-level approximation)*
- **Current administration:** Rural ridge area near Weston, Lewis County, West Virginia.
- **Historical names/boundaries:** appears in family-history and settlement descriptions more as a ridge/locality than as a formal town.

## Historical Context (1800s-1900s)
Loveberry Ridge matters in this project as part of the Catholic and settlement landscape around the Copley farm area. It is one of the place names that helps readers picture how farm, village, and church geography related to one another in southwestern Lewis County.

- **Religious context:** site of **St. Bernard's Catholic Church** in the family-history narrative.
- **Settlement context:** part of the broader **Camden / Cove Lick / Loveberry** cluster used in the Murray Settlement framework.
- **Landscape role:** a nearby ridge/church location rather than the main farm tract itself.

## Copley Family Connection
- The raw Part 1 family-history narrative identifies **Michael Copley Sr.**'s gravestone at **St. Bernard's Catholic Church** on Loveberry Ridge.
- The same narrative says St. Bernard's stood about **two miles** from the Copley farm / village area by dirt road, making it a recurring landmark in the Lewis County family story.
- Loveberry also appears in the research vocabulary around the early Irish Catholic settlement, especially when testing whether deeds or parish records fall inside the **Cove Lick / Camden / Loveberry / St. Michael's** zone.
