# Cove Lick, West Virginia

## Geographic Information
- 🗺️ **Google Maps:** [View on Google Maps](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cove+Lick+Road+Weston+West+Virginia)
- **Approx. coordinates (Copley Road/Cove Lick area):** 39.05, -80.49
- **Current administration:** Rural locality near Weston, Lewis County, West Virginia.
- **Historical names/boundaries:** appears in road/tract descriptions more than as a formal town name.

## Historical Context (1800s-1900s)
Cove Lick represents land-based settlement geography: roads, tract lines, and deed descriptions rather than municipal addresses. For immigrant farm families, this was the practical center of household continuity.

- **Economy:** farm production with later proximity to oil-era activity.
- **Record style:** deeds, plats, metes-and-bounds descriptions.
- **Significance:** transition from wage labor to rooted land tenure.
- **Family-history placement:** the narrative places the 1843 Copley-Hoffman tract at what is now the intersection of `Copley Road` and `Cove Lick Road`.

## Figure-Backed Local Detail
- The family-history narrative reports that the 1843 agreement covered `200 acres` and that the tract formed part of a larger `500-acre` Commonwealth of Virginia grant to [[People/Weeden Hoffman|Weeden Hoffman]] dated `December 30, 1842`.
- The same source says the installment payments were not fully completed until `February 24, 1881`, when Michael and son [[People/John Copley|John Copley]] finished paying Hoffman heirs.
- Figures 8 and 9 in the Part 1 history reportedly reproduce the `1843` agreement and an `1865` map of the farm, making Cove Lick one of the best visually documented early settlement places in the project.
- Later figures tied to the `1900` oil strike describe a small village of Copley growing around the farm.
- That same narrative says John Copley gave land for a local grammar school and for an Evangelical United Brethren church, while St. Bernard's on [[Places/Loveberry Ridge West Virginia|Loveberry Ridge]] stood about two miles away by dirt road.
- It also preserves a small built-environment detail that a gas station with convenience store once served the village before disappearing.

## Figure Provenance
- `COPLEY HISTORY PART 1 final 2.pdf`, **page 11, Fig. 8**: Weeden Hoffman's `1843` agreement to sell Patrick and Michael Copley `200 AC`. This is documentary evidence for the settlement transaction, not just an illustration.
- `COPLEY HISTORY PART 1 final 2.pdf`, **page 12, Fig. 9**: `1865` map of Michael Copley's `200 AC` farm made for Hoffman's heirs. This is the main figure-backed support for the tract-geometry language on this page.
- `COPLEY HISTORY PART 1 final 2.pdf`, **pages 18-21, Figs. 16-21**: the Copley No. 1 well, historical marker, village of Copley, grammar school, EUB church, and St. Bernard's image set used for the later oil-village / school / church landscape description.

The deed and map images on this page are evidence-bearing figures. The later village, school, church, and well figures are better treated as settlement-landscape context unless and until each one is independently tied to external records.
