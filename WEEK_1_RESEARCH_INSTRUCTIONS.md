## 📋 Task 1: Lewis County Deed Records Search (RQ-M1)

**Objective:** Find the first Murray family land purchase in Lewis County, WV (1825–1855). This establishes whether Murrays were the settlement anchor.

**Time Estimate:** 2–3 hours  
**Difficulty:** Medium (database navigation)  
**Key Finding:** Any deed showing a Murray purchasing land in Cove Lick/Camden/Loveberry area BEFORE 1843 = strong evidence of anchor family role

---

### **Step 1: Access FamilySearch Lewis County Deeds**

1. Go to: https://www.familysearch.org/
2. Click **"Search"** at top
3. Under "By Location," search for: **"Lewis County, West Virginia"**
4. In results, look for: **"West Virginia, Lewis County Land Records, 1817–[year]"**
5. Click that collection
6. You should see options for "Browse" and "Search"

**Alternative (if FamilySearch access limited):**
- Try: https://www.ancestry.com/ → Search "Lewis County, West Virginia Deeds"
- May require paid subscription, but 14-day free trial available

---

### **Step 2: Set Search Filters**

Once in the Lewis County deeds collection:

**Search Parameters:**
- **Surname:** MURRAY (try variations: MURRY, MOREY, MURY if no results)
- **Given Name:** (leave blank to catch all first names)
- **Date Range:** 1825–1855
- **Search Type:** Exact match (if available), or name match

**Do NOT filter by:**
- Specific townland (we want to see ALL Murray purchases)
- Specific person (we're looking for any Murray)

---

### **Step 3: Record All Murray Results**

For EACH deed record found, record:

```
DEED RECORD #[number]:
- Person Name: ________________
- Date: ________________
- Property Location: ________________
- Acreage/Details: ________________
- Transaction Type (Bought/Sold/Leased): ________________
- Notes: ________________
```

**Key locations to watch for:**
- Cove Lick
- Camden  
- Loveberry
- Copley Road / Copley area
- Any nearby townland

---

### **Step 4: Cross-Check Against Known Copley Purchase**

**Baseline for comparison:**
- Michael Copley Sr. + Patrick Copley purchased from **Weeden Hoffman** in **1843**
- Location: Cove Lick area, Lewis County

**Question:** Did any Murray purchase land BEFORE 1843 in the same area? If YES → Murray is anchor. If NO → Murray role unclear.

---

### **Step 5: Expand Search (if time permits)**

If you find Murray deeds, also search:
- **WEEDEN HOFFMAN** — Did he sell to multiple Irish Catholic families? (Indicates broker role)
- **DOLAN** — Any Dolan purchases in same timeframe/area?
- **HANNON, HANLEY, MULLOOLY** — Same check

## 🚢 Task 2: *Powhatan* Ship Manifest Search (RQ-M2)

**Objective:** Find full *Powhatan* (Aug 20, 1838) passenger list. Look for settlement family surnames (Murray, Dolan, Hanley, Hannon, Mullooly, Reynolds) traveling with Copleys.

**Time Estimate:** 1–2 hours  
**Difficulty:** Low (straightforward search)  
**Key Finding:** Multiple settlement families on same ship = organized group emigration

**What we know:**
- Ship: ***Powhatan***
- Date: **August 20, 1838**
- Known passengers: **Michael Copely** (age 22) and **Pat Copely** (age 20)
- Spelling: "Copely" (Catholic variant)

---

### **Step 1: Access Ship Manifest Records**

**Option A: FamilySearch (Recommended)**
1. Go to: https://www.familysearch.org/
2. Click **"Search"**
3. Under "By Record Type," click **"Passenger Lists"**
4. Search: **Ship Name: Powhatan** + **Year: 1838**
5. Filter for August 1838 arrival

**Option B: Ancestry.com**
1. Go to: https://www.ancestry.com/
2. Search **"Ship Manifests"**
3. Search: **Powhatan 1838**
4. Look for August 20, 1838 arrival

**Option C: NARA (National Archives)**
- NARA M237 index (may require visiting archive or requesting copies)
- Less user-friendly but comprehensive

---

### **Step 2: Locate the *Powhatan* August 1838 Manifest**

When you find multiple ship results:
- Look for **August 20, 1838** specifically
- Should show arrival port (likely **New York**)
- Verify ship name: ***Powhatan***

If multiple voyages exist, check:
- Departure port (Ireland, UK, or other)
- Vessel type (usually listed as "ship")
- Passenger count (~100–300 typical for this era)

---

### **Step 3: Search for Known Passengers**

Once you have the manifest page(s):

**First, verify the Copleys are there:**
- Search for: **"Michael Copely"** or **"Michael Copley"** — age 22
- Search for: **"Pat Copely"** or **"Patrick Copley"** — age 20
- If found: ✅ You have the right manifest

**Then search for other settlement family surnames:**
- **MURRAY** (any first name)
- **DOLAN**
- **HANLEY** / **HANNY**
- **HANNON**
- **MULLOOLY** / **MULLALLY** / **MULLOY**
- **REYNOLDS**
- **GIBBONS** (Bridget Gibbons married Michael Copely of Fairymount 1864)

---

### **Step 4: Record All Matching Names**

For EACH family name found on the manifest, record:

```
PASSENGER RECORD #[number]:
- Name: ________________
- Age: ________________
- Occupation (if listed): ________________
- Destination (if listed): ________________
- Origin/Where from (if listed): ________________
- Notes: ________________
```

---

### **Step 5: Identify Patterns**

**Questions to ask:**
- How many settlement family members are on this ship?
- Do they travel together (family groups) or separately?
- Do they list the same destination?
- Any notation about "following" or "joining" other family members?

---

### **Step 6: Cross-Check with *Kutusoff* (1837)**

If you have time:
1. Repeat steps 1–4 for ***Kutusoff*** **1837** voyage
2. Look for: Bridget Copley, Mary Copely, other siblings of Michael Sr.
3. See if *Kutusoff* passengers overlap with *Powhatan* passengers

## 🇮🇪 Task 3: Griffith's Valuation Searches (RQ-M3, RQ-M7)

**Objective:** Complete the Roscommon estate-mate clustering verification. Search for Dolan, Mullooly, Reynolds family names in Griffith's Valuation (1857).

**Time Estimate:** 1–2 hours  
**Difficulty:** Low (website is straightforward)  
**Key Finding:** Multiple families on same Sandford estate = verified estate-mate community

**What we already know (verified):**
- Copleys: Sandford estate, Ballindrumlea (Kilkeevin)
- Murrays: Multiple entries, Sandford estate
- Hanleys: Sandford estate, Ballindrumlea
- Hannons: Sandford estate

**Still need to verify:**
- Dolans (partially done, may need to expand)
- Mulloolys / Mulrooneys
- Reynolds

---

### **Step 1: Access Griffith's Valuation Online**

**Best source: askaboutireland.ie (FREE)**

1. Go to: https://www.askaboutireland.ie/griffith-valuation/
2. Click **"Search"**
3. You should see dropdown menus for:
   - County
   - Baronies
   - Parishes
   - Townlands

**Alternative sources:**
- https://www.irishgenealogyhub.com/ (searchable database)
- https://registers.nli.ie/ (National Library of Ireland)

---

### **Step 2: Set Search Parameters**

1. **County:** ROSCOMMON
2. **Baronies:** Select all (or focus on Castlereagh if dropdown available)
3. **Parishes:** Search each of these individually:
   - **KILGEFIN**
   - **KILKEEVIN**
   - **KILCORKEY**
4. **Townlands:** Leave blank (search entire parish)

## 💡 Tips & Troubleshooting

### If you can't find records:
- **Try spelling variations:** Dolan/Delin, Mullooly/Mullally/Mulloy, Hanley/Hanny
- **Expand date range:** Try 1850–1860 if 1857 alone isn't working
- **Check adjacent parishes:** Ballagh (aka Kilgefin), Kilroosky nearby
- **Ask Claude for Chrome:** "I can't find X surname, should I expand search to...?"

### If you find too many results:
- **Filter by townland:** Focus on Sandford estate, Ballindrumlea, Fairymount first
- **Record key fields only:** Name, townland, landlord (don't need every detail)
- **Ask Claude:** "I found 50 Dolan entries, should I focus on specific townlands?"

### If databases are slow or unavailable:
- **Try alternative sources:** Move to next task, come back later
- **Report to Claude:** "FamilySearch is slow, moving to askaboutireland.ie"
- **Use offline notes:** Record findings by hand, transcribe to Claude later
