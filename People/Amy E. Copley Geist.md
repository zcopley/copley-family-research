## Biographical Narrative

Amy grew up in a household deeply connected to engineering, professional societies, and international travel. The appendix biography emphasizes that traveling with her parents to engineering meetings and being around engineers from many countries helped shape her interest in global cultures from an early age. French au pairs in Hinsdale also helped spark her long-term interest in French language and Francophone cultures.

The appendix sketch gives her Hinsdale years a more specific texture than the current summary alone suggests. It describes walks with her parents through Fullersburg Woods and past Graue Mill, neighborhood friendships, youth soccer, piano and French horn lessons, and annual California family reunions at the Lawrence Welk Resort in Escondido. That combination of local routine, travel, and extended-family contact helps explain why her later page reads as both internationally oriented and strongly networked through family.

Her childhood was divided between Hinsdale, Illinois, and State College, Pennsylvania. After the family moved to State College in 2002 for [[Judith Ann Todd Copley|Judy Todd Copley]]'s Penn State role, Amy built a new social world through school and soccer, especially through friendships begun at Mount Nittany Middle School and continued at State College Area High School. The appendix presents that move not just as a relocation, but as the point where she turned an initially reluctant change into a durable Pennsylvania base that later led her to Penn State.

At Penn State, Amy studied French and international politics while building the combination of language skill, travel, and development interest that shaped her later work. Her study abroad in Lille and Dakar, followed by later research exposure in Kenya and Rwanda, pushed her toward the overlap of politics and economic development. The London School of Economics then expanded that path through both formal study and family connection: the appendix notes that her LSE year also gave her a chance to spend time with English relatives and renewed her friendship with Nathan Geist through London pub-trivia nights before their later relationship began.

After graduate study at the London School of Economics, Amy moved to Washington, DC, where she worked at Brookings before joining the World Bank's Africa Gender Innovation Lab. Her appendix sketch connects that work to a broader interest in evidence-based development programming, gender gaps, and policy impact in Sub-Saharan Africa, while also showing continuity with the research-and-institutions culture of the Stephen line.

The appendix also supplies a cleaner bridge into her present-day family life. It traces the relationship with Nathan from renewed dating in 2017 through Remy the dog, the 2020 move to Silver Spring at the start of the COVID-19 period, the 2021 Upper Marlboro house purchase and marriage in State College, and the birth of [[William S. Geist|William Stephen Geist]] in December 2023. It also notes her more recent interest in pollinator gardening and backyard beekeeping.
