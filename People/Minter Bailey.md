# Minter Bailey

## Biographical Profile

[[Minter Bailey]] appears in Appendix 2 of the family history as one member of Camden, Bailey and Camden, the land-speculation group tied to the proposed origin of the Copley farm. The appendix identifies him as a surveyor and owner of the Bailey Hotel / Bailey House in Weston.

## Land-Chain Role

Appendix 2 says [[Octavius Pickering]] assigned claims to [[Gideon D. Camden]], [[Richard P. Camden]], and Minter Bailey. The same appendix proposes that [[People/Weeden Hoffman|Weeden Hoffman]] obtained the warrant behind his 500-acre 1842 grant through this group before selling part of that land to [[People/Michael Copley Sr|Michael Copley Sr.]] and [[People/Patrick Copley|Patrick Copley]] in 1843.

Bailey's reported surveying background is especially relevant: if verified, he may help explain how large claims were located, subdivided, and converted into sellable Lewis County tracts.
