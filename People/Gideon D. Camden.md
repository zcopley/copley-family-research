# Gideon D. Camden

## Biographical Profile

[[Gideon D. Camden]] appears in Appendix 2 of the family history as one of the local land speculators in the proposed chain behind the Copley farm. The appendix identifies him as a lawyer, judge, and politician who lived near [[Places/Weston West Virginia|Weston]].

## Land-Chain Role

Appendix 2 describes Camden, Bailey and Camden — [[Gideon D. Camden]], [[Richard P. Camden]], and [[Minter Bailey]] — as the group to whom [[Octavius Pickering]] assigned claims from the Pickering land interest. The same appendix proposes that [[People/Weeden Hoffman|Weeden Hoffman]] may have obtained the warrant for his 500-acre December 1842 grant through this group.

For Copley research, Gideon D. Camden matters less as a family member than as part of the land-market machinery that may have converted a large speculative claim into the smaller farm tract sold to [[People/Michael Copley Sr|Michael Copley Sr.]] and [[People/Patrick Copley|Patrick Copley]] in 1843.
