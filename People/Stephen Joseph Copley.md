# Stephen Joseph Copley (1970–2023)

📊 View [[Family Tree]] for visual context.

## Biographical Profile

[[Stephen Joseph Copley]] was born **May 1, 1970** at Yale-New Haven Hospital, New Haven, Connecticut — the seventh and youngest child of [[Stephen Michael Copley]] and [[Marcia Thornton Copley]]. Shortly after his birth, the family relocated to Southern California when his father accepted a position at USC.

He was diagnosed early in life with autism and significant developmental disabilities. As he grew older, he needed substantial daily support, was nonverbal, and could wander away if not closely supervised. His care profoundly shaped the entire Copley family and directly influenced the careers and life paths of several relatives.

- **Birth:** May 1, 1970, Yale-New Haven Hospital, New Haven, Connecticut
- **Death:** June 10, 2023 (in his sleep, age 53)
- **Burial:** San Juan Bautista, CA, August 8, 2023
- **Care context:** Harbor Regional Center client; weekday care at the Intercommunity Exceptional Children's Home, Long Beach, CA; returned home on weekends during childhood
- **Later care:** Became a full-time client / ward of the State of California after his parents divorced in 1983

## Biographical Narrative

Stephen Joseph's appendix biography was written by his father, [[Stephen Michael Copley]]. It presents his life as both a personal family story and part of the broader history of developmental-disability services in California after the Frank Lanterman Developmental Disabilities Act of 1969.

His birth also marks a precise transition point in the family story. Shortly after he was born at Yale-New Haven Hospital, the family moved west from [[Places/Madison Connecticut|Madison, Connecticut]] to California when his father joined USC. After a brief rental period in Lomita, the household settled at `4029 Via Nivel` in [[Places/Palos Verdes Estates California|Palos Verdes Estates]], creating the Southern California home base that would shape the younger Stephen-line siblings.

The family first cared for Stephen at home in Palos Verdes Estates. As his support needs grew, Harbor Regional Center placed him during the week at the Intercommunity Exceptional Children's Home in Long Beach, with weekend visits home. His father describes him as nonverbal, in need of constant supervision, and prone to wandering, which made daily family life unusually demanding in a household that also included six older school-age children.

That pressure did not only create hardship; it redirected the family. Stephen's needs helped pull [[Marcia Thornton Copley|Marcia]] toward formal work in developmental-disability services after she completed a bachelor's degree in human services at Cal State Dominguez Hills. The appendix notes that she became the first parent of a Harbor Regional Center client to be hired there as a Client Service Coordinator. Later, through her marriage to Ed Dawson, the family's connection to Social Vocational Services deepened further, and many of Stephen's siblings worked in that world at one time or another.

In branch terms, Stephen Joseph's page is therefore about more than one individual life. He stands at the center of a major redirection in the Stephen-line story: away from a simple academic-family narrative and toward a long family involvement with disability care, caregiving institutions, and support work in California. After Stephen and Marcia divorced in 1983, he became a full-time Harbor Regional Center client and ward of the State of California, but remained a lasting point of emotional connection across the wider family.

Stephen died in his sleep on June 10, 2023. He was buried at San Juan Bautista, California, on August 8, 2023. A Celebration of Life ceremony was held September 7, 2023, at Skylinks Golf Course in Long Beach, attended by caregivers, friends from SVS, and family members in California and by Zoom.

> *"Stephen taught us all a new meaning of love. Through Stephen, we met other young children, individuals with disabilities and their parents, who otherwise we never would have known."* — Stephen Michael Copley
