# Octavius Pickering

## Biographical Profile

[[Octavius Pickering]] appears in Appendix 2 of the family history as the heir or successor to the Pickering land interest behind the proposed Copley farm land chain. The appendix identifies him as a Boston attorney who, like his father, did not live in Virginia.

## Land-Chain Role

Appendix 2 says Octavius assigned claims to land speculators [[Gideon D. Camden]], [[Richard P. Camden]], and [[Minter Bailey]], operating as Camden, Bailey and Camden. That proposed assignment matters because it may explain how [[People/Weeden Hoffman|Weeden Hoffman]] obtained the land warrant or claim behind the 500-acre Commonwealth of Virginia grant dated December 30, 1842.

If verified, Octavius is part of the legal chain between a large federal / Virginia land-grant framework and the local Lewis County transactions that made the Copley farm possible.
