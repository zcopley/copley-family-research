# Thomas Copley Jr. (c. 1580–1647)

## Biographical Profile

- **Birth:** c. 1580 (estimated)
- **Death:** 1647, Bredon's Norton, Worcestershire, England
- **Status:** Eldest son of [[People/Thomas Copley Sr.|Thomas Copley Sr.]]; lord of Bredon's Norton manor
- **Seat:** Bredon's Norton, Worcestershire
- **Documented:** Present at the 1634 Visitation of Worcestershire (listed as #1 son)

## Biographical Summary

Thomas Copley Jr. inherited Bredon's Norton manor from his father and held the estate through the English Civil War era. He was present at the 1634 Visitation of Worcestershire, where his family is documented in detail. Following his death in 1647, the manor was sold in 1649, ending the family's direct presence in the village and leaving his younger sons without inheritance — a circumstance that directly contributed to his brother [[People/Captain John Copley|Captain John Copley]]'s emigration to Ireland in 1657.

## Notable Events

**1634 — Visitation of Worcestershire:** Present at the 1634 Visitation of Worcestershire (listed as #1 eldest son), confirming his status as lord of Bredon's Norton at that date.

**1647 — Death:** Died in 1647, at the height of the English Civil War, leaving the estate in uncertain hands during political upheaval.

**1649 — Manor Sale:** The Bredon's Norton manor was sold two years after his death, ending the family's 16th–17th-century seat in Worcestershire.
