# Weeden Hoffman

## Biographical Profile

[[Weeden Hoffman]] was a Lewis County, Virginia landowner and the counterparty in the pivotal **January 5, 1843 land deed** that established the Copley family's permanent settlement in what became West Virginia. On that date, Hoffman sold a **200-acre tract** in the Cove Lick/Copley Road area near [[Places/Weston West Virginia|Weston]] to [[Michael Copley Sr|Michael Copley]] and his brother [[Patrick Copley]].

This transaction is the single most important documentary anchor in the Copley family's American history, marking the transition from immigrant laborers on the B&O Railroad corridor to settled landowners in Lewis County.

## Historical Significance

- The deed is dated **January 5, 1843** and recorded in Lewis County deed books.
- The tract became known as the **Copley farm** and remained in the family for generations.
- The area around it became known as **Copley Road**, a place name that persists to this day.

## Land-Chain Context

Appendix 2 of the family history preserves a working explanation for how Hoffman may have obtained the land later sold to Michael and Patrick. The appendix says a large land bonus grant near Weston passed from [[Thomas Pickering]] / Pickering family interests to [[Octavius Pickering]], then into claims assigned to Camden, Bailey and Camden: [[Gideon D. Camden]], [[Richard P. Camden]], and [[Minter Bailey]].

The family records reportedly include a Commonwealth of Virginia grant to Weeden Hoffman dated **December 30, 1842**, for **500 acres**. Appendix 2 proposes that Hoffman may have obtained a land warrant through Camden, Bailey and Camden, possibly through his business relationship with Richard P. Camden, and then claimed the land from the Commonwealth of Virginia Land Clerk. The Copley 200-acre tract was then sold out of that larger 500-acre holding in January 1843.

This makes Hoffman more than a one-off deed counterparty. If the appendix land-chain interpretation is correct, he sits at the practical interface between Virginia land grants, local land speculation, and immigrant landholding in the Cove Lick / Weston area.
