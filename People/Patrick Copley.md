# Patrick Copley (c.1818–unknown)

📊 View [[Family Tree]] for visual context.

## Biographical Profile

[[Patrick Copley]] is the brother of [[Michael Copley Sr|Michael Copley]], the immigrant patriarch of the West Virginia Copley line. Patrick emigrated from Ireland alongside Michael and co-established the family's first land holding in [[Places/Lewis County West Virginia|Lewis County, West Virginia]].

- **Birth:** c.1818, [[Places/Kilgefin Ireland|Kilgefin]], [[Places/County Roscommon Ireland|County Roscommon]], Ireland (inferred from age on 1838 passenger manifest)
- **Death:** unknown
- **Emigration:** August 20, 1838 — arrived New York aboard the *Powhatan*, listed as "Pat Copely," age 20, alongside "Michael Copely," age 22

## Key Events

- **1838:** Arrived New York on the *Powhatan* with brother Michael.
- **1843:** Co-signed the Weeden Hoffman land deed with Michael — a 200-acre purchase in what became the [[Places/Cove Lick West Virginia|Copley Road/Cove Lick area]] near [[Places/Weston West Virginia|Weston, WV]]. This deed established the family's permanent settlement in Lewis County.
- **Post-1843:** Patrick's subsequent life is undocumented. It is unknown whether he remained in Lewis County, moved elsewhere, or died young.
