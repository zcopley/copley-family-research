# William Copley of Woolbedding (c. 1450–1500s)

## Biographical Profile

- **Born:** c. 1450
- **Died:** late 15th–early 16th century (exact date unknown)
- **Location:** Woolbedding (Wool Bedding), Sussex, England; also listed as Enfylde
- **Seat:** Woolbedding, Sussex
- **Significance:** Father of Thomas Copley Sr. of Bredon; ancestor of all American Copleys through the Bredon line

## Family Relationships

### Parents

- **Father:** [[People/Roger Copley|Roger Copley of Roughley]] (1430–1490)
- **Mother:** Jane Anne Hoo (b. 1448)

### Marriage

- **Wife:** Bennett (Bennet) Chaloner of Enfylde
- **Significance of marriage:** The Chaloner family of Enfylde (later known as enfolds, Worcestershire area) was a prominent family; Bennett's dowry or family connections likely brought land or status that enhanced the Copley standing

### Children

1. **Thomas Copley Sr.** (c. 1540–1593) — married Margaret Newport and Elenor Midelmore; patriarch of the Bredon Copley line (see [[People/Thomas Copley Sr.|Thomas Copley Sr.]])
2. Possibly other children (records unclear)

## Historical Context

William Copley lived during the Wars of the Roses (1455–1487) and the Tudor consolidation of power. The Woolbedding seat in Sussex placed William in the heart of the southern English gentry network. His marriage to Bennett Chaloner created alliance bonds with another prominent Sussex family.

The transition from the Roughley seat (Surrey) to Woolbedding (Sussex) in William's generation suggests a strategic relocation or inheritance of new estates. Woolbedding became the established Copley seat for this branch and remained important until the family's later move to Worcestershire.

## Verification

**Documented in:**
- **1569 Visitation of Worcestershire** — William is listed as the father of Thomas Copley Sr., with his wife Bennett Chaloner named. This is the primary verification source.
- **1634 Visitation of Worcestershire** — Genealogies extending from William through Thomas Sr. and Thomas Jr.

The 1569 Visitation confirms William Copley's existence and his marriage to Bennett Chaloner.

## Research Status

Documentation is sparse for William himself but solid for his connection to Thomas Copley Sr. The Visitations confirm him as a real historical person, though details of his life remain limited.
