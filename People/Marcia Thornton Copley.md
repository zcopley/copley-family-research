# Marcia Thornton Copley (first wife of Stephen Michael Copley)

📊 View [[Family Tree]] for visual context.

## Biographical Profile

[[Marcia Thornton Copley]] was the first wife of [[Stephen Michael Copley]], whom she married while both were students at UC Berkeley. She bore seven children and managed the household largely alone as Stephen pursued his research career. After the challenges of raising a severely disabled child strained the marriage, she and Stephen divorced in 1983. She later earned a college degree, launched a career in developmental disabilities services, and remarried.

- **First marriage:** [[Stephen Michael Copley]] (married at UC Berkeley; divorced 1983)
- **Second marriage:** Edward (Ed) Dawson — founder of **Social Vocational Services (SVS)**, a non-profit serving the developmentally disabled
- **Education:** BA in Human Services, Cal State Dominguez Hills (completed after divorce; education had been interrupted when she married Stephen)
- **Career:** Client Service Coordinator, Harbor Regional Center — notably the first parent of a client to apply for and be employed by the Harbor Regional Center; dedicated her career to helping the developmentally disabled and their families

## Notes

- Her son [[Stephen Joseph Copley]] was diagnosed as autistic and severely developmentally disabled; his care profoundly shaped the family and directly influenced Marcia's career path.
- SVS (Social Vocational Services), founded by her second husband Ed Dawson in 1973, grew to serve thousands of individuals at 80+ locations throughout California. Several of Stephen's children also worked for SVS at various points.
- Marcia is a descendant (through her mother's side) of **Patrick Breen**, a survivor of the ill-fated Donner Party. Patrick established a ranch near Mission San Juan Bautista — where Stephen Joseph was buried in 2023.
