# Marcia Thornton Copley (first wife of Stephen Michael Copley)

📊 View [[Family Tree]] for visual context.

## Biographical Profile

[[Marcia Thornton Copley]] was the first wife of [[Stephen Michael Copley]], whom she married while both were students at UC Berkeley. She bore seven children and carried much of the household and child-care load as Stephen pursued his research career. After the challenges of raising a child with significant developmental disabilities placed great strain on the family, she and Stephen divorced in 1983. She later earned a college degree, launched a career in developmental-disability services, and remarried.

- **First marriage:** [[Stephen Michael Copley]] (married at UC Berkeley; divorced 1983)
- **Second marriage:** Edward (Ed) Dawson — founder of **Social Vocational Services (SVS)**, a non-profit serving people with developmental disabilities
- **Education:** BA in Human Services, Cal State Dominguez Hills (completed after divorce; education had been interrupted when she married Stephen)
- **Career:** Client Service Coordinator, Harbor Regional Center — notably the first parent of a client to apply for and be employed by the Harbor Regional Center; dedicated her career to helping people with developmental disabilities and their families

## Biographical Narrative

Marcia appears in the appendix primarily through Stephen Michael Copley's and Stephen Joseph Copley's sketches. She married Stephen while he was a UC Berkeley student, then helped raise a rapidly growing family through Berkeley, Madison, Connecticut, and Palos Verdes Estates. During those years Stephen's research and university work often demanded intense time and attention, while Marcia carried much of the daily household work.

Her youngest son [[Stephen Joseph Copley]] changed the course of the family's life. After his diagnosis and placement through Harbor Regional Center, Marcia returned to complete her education at Cal State Dominguez Hills and then became a Client Service Coordinator at Harbor Regional Center. The appendix emphasizes that she was the first parent of a client to apply for and be employed by the center.

Marcia later married Ed Dawson, founder of Social Vocational Services. Through that connection, SVS became an important recurring institution in the Stephen-line family story, employing or serving multiple family members over time.

## Notes

- Her son [[Stephen Joseph Copley]] was diagnosed with autism and significant developmental disabilities; his care profoundly shaped the family and directly influenced Marcia's career path.
- SVS (Social Vocational Services), founded by her second husband Ed Dawson in 1973, grew to serve thousands of individuals at 80+ locations throughout California. Several of Stephen's children also worked for SVS at various points.
- Marcia is a descendant (through her mother's side) of **Patrick Breen**, a survivor of the ill-fated Donner Party. Patrick established a ranch near Mission San Juan Bautista — where Stephen Joseph was buried in 2023.
