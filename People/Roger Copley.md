# Roger Copley of Roughley (1430–1490)

## Biographical Profile

- **Born:** 1430
- **Died:** 1490
- **Location:** Roughley (also Roughey), Surrey, England
- **Title:** Esquire
- **Significance:** Son of Sir Richard Copley; father of William Copley who established the Bredon Copley line; established Copley seat at Roughley, Surrey

## Family Relationships

### Parents

- **Father:** [[People/Sir Richard Copley|Sir Richard Copley]] (c. 1375–1434)
- **Mother:** Elizabeth Harrington (1404–1456)

### Marriage

- **Wife:** Jane Anne Hoo (b. 1448)
- **Married:** mid-15th century
- **Jane Anne's family:** The Hoo family of Kent; connection to the Newport family (through whom the Copleys gain a distant relationship to the Raleigh family)

### Children

From Roger Copley + Jane Anne Hoo:

1. **Sir Roger Copley** — continued the Roughley estate line
2. **Richard Copley** — collateral line
3. **William Copley** (b.c. 1450–1490s) — married Bennett Chaloner of Enfylde; **ancestor of all American Copleys through the Bredon line** (see [[People/William Copley (Woolbedding)|William Copley of Woolbedding]])
4. **Six daughters:** Anne, Eleanor, Dorothy, Margaret, Elizabeth, Ada

## Historical Significance

Roger Copley's marriage to Jane Anne Hoo connected the Copleys to prominent Kent and South England families. This marriage created a network relationship that placed the Copleys in contact with the Hoo, Newport, and eventually (through distant cousinship) the Raleigh families—connections that facilitated the Copleys' rise to prominence in English gentry society during the 15th and 16th centuries.

The choice of William as the ancestor of the main line (rather than the eldest Roger) suggests that William's marriage to Bennett Chaloner may have brought land or status advantages that made that branch more prominent.

## Seat

**Roughley, Surrey** — Roger Copley's documented seat, inherited from his father. The property remained in the Copley family for generations, passing through the elder Roger's line.
