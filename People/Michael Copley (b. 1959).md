[[Michael Copley (b. 1959)]] is the oldest son of [[Stephen Michael Copley]] and his first wife [[Marcia Thornton Copley]]. ## Biographical Narrative

Michael Stephen Copley belongs to the first wave of Stephen Copley's children and is the clearest living link back to the family's Berkeley graduate-school years. He was born in **1959** while his father was still pursuing graduate study at UC Berkeley, before the 1964 move to Connecticut and before the family expanded to its final seven-child first-marriage shape. The Stephen sketch places Michael among the first four children born during the years when Stephen was balancing research and graduate work while [[Marcia Thornton Copley|Marcia]] carried much of the day-to-day childcare burden.

His early family geography moved from Berkeley to [[Places/Madison Connecticut|Madison, Connecticut]], where the family bought its first house on Long Island Sound. That Madison period matters because it marks the household's first settled family stage: church and community involvement, a growing sibling set, and the family culture that later siblings remembered as stable and happy before the Southern California move.

As the oldest child in the sibling cohort, Michael also occupies a slightly different role from Sara, Paul, Peter, Susan, and the younger children. He would have experienced the 1970 relocation to [[Places/Palos Verdes Estates California|Palos Verdes Estates]] not as early childhood but as the beginning of his teenage years. The Stephen sketch remembers Palos Verdes as a period of patio barbecues, beach life, Disneyland trips, coaching, and eastern Sierra camping and backpacking trips. Those memories belong to the whole sibling group more than to Michael alone, but they help place him inside the household culture that shaped the first USC-era generation of Stephen-line descendants.

The appendix-backed record remains thinner for Michael individually than for some siblings: there is no separate first-person sketch for him in the appendix, and the current page still relies on family-context references for schooling, residence, marriage, and children. The Stephen sketch does, however, make one broader point that helps anchor him in branch history: all of Stephen and Marcia's children except [[Stephen Joseph Copley]] attended USC or associated universities using the faculty tuition benefit. That places Michael squarely in the first USC-linked generation of descendants, even though his exact course of study and later career still need fuller documentation.
