[[Michael Copley (b. 1959)]] is the oldest son of [[Stephen Michael Copley]] and his first wife [[Marcia Thornton Copley]]. As the eldest of Stephen's seven children from his first marriage, Michael was born during his father's early career at Pratt & Whitney Aircraft.

## Biographical Narrative

Michael Stephen Copley belongs to the first wave of Stephen Copley's California-and-Connecticut children. He was born in **1959** while his father was still a Berkeley graduate student and early academic, making him the eldest child in the family before the move east for Pratt & Whitney work. The Stephen sketch places Michael among the first four children born during the years when Stephen was balancing graduate study, research, and the start of his professional career, while [[Marcia Thornton Copley|Marcia]] carried much of the day-to-day childcare burden.

His early family geography moved from Berkeley to [[Places/Madison Connecticut|Madison, Connecticut]], where the family bought its first house on Long Island Sound. That Madison period now reads as the stable childhood stage before the larger Southern California move: church and community involvement, public-school years, and the growing household that eventually included seven children in Stephen and Marcia's first family.

In **1970** the family relocated to [[Places/Palos Verdes Estates California|Palos Verdes Estates]] when Stephen joined USC. For Michael, that meant adolescence in the Los Angeles-area branch of the family story rather than in the earlier Berkeley or Connecticut settings. The Stephen sketch remembers Palos Verdes as a period of patio barbecues, beach life, Disneyland trips, coaching, and camping/backpacking trips in the eastern Sierra. Those details belong to the whole sibling group more than to Michael alone, but they help place him in the household culture that shaped the older Stephen-line children before college.

The appendix-backed record remains thinner for Michael individually than for some siblings: there is no separate first-person sketch for him in the appendix, and the current page still relies on family-context references for schooling, residence, marriage, and children. Even so, the Stephen sketch makes clear that Michael's life course belongs to the first USC-era generation of descendants who came of age after the family had shifted decisively from the Michael Joseph scientific household into the wider California branch.
