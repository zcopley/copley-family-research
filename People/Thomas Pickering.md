# Thomas Pickering

## Biographical Profile

[[Thomas Pickering]] appears in Appendix 2 of the family history as the original large-grant holder in the proposed land chain behind the Copley farm. The appendix says a **20,000-acre land bonus grant near Weston** was made to Pickering for early national service, and that he never lived in Virginia.

**Identity caution:** Appendix 2 names "Thomas Pickering," but the offices it lists — Secretary of State, Secretary of War, and Postmaster General under George Washington / John Adams — correspond to **Timothy Pickering** in standard U.S. history references. This page preserves the current vault link as "Thomas Pickering" while flagging the likely name correction for future cleanup.

## Land-Chain Role

According to Appendix 2, Pickering's interest in the land passed to his son [[Octavius Pickering]], who assigned claims to the land-speculation group Camden, Bailey and Camden: [[Gideon D. Camden]], [[Richard P. Camden]], and [[Minter Bailey]]. That chain is relevant because [[People/Weeden Hoffman|Weeden Hoffman]] reportedly received a Commonwealth of Virginia grant for 500 acres in December 1842, shortly before selling 200 acres to [[People/Michael Copley Sr|Michael Copley Sr.]] and [[People/Patrick Copley|Patrick Copley]] in January 1843.
