# Judith Ann Todd Copley ("Judy") (second wife of Stephen Michael Copley)

📊 View [[Family Tree]] for visual context.

## Biographical Profile

[[Judith Ann Todd Copley]] ("Judy") is the second wife of [[Stephen Michael Copley]], whom she married in 1984. A distinguished materials scientist and academic administrator in her own right, she and Stephen are noted as the only husband and wife to both serve as Presidents of ASM International (ASMI), 30 years apart.

- **Origin:** Wakefield, Yorkshire, England
- **Marriage:** [[Stephen Michael Copley]], 1984 (14 years his junior; they met when she joined the Materials Science Department at USC)
- **Current location:** State College, Pennsylvania area
- **Occupation:** Professor, Engineering Science and Mechanics Department, Penn State University (PSU)

## Education

- **PhD** — Cambridge University
- Post-doctoral research: Imperial College of Science and Technology, London; State University of New York at Stonybrook; UC Berkeley

## Career

**Notable roles and honors:**
- President of the Society of Engineering Science (SES) — elected Fellow of SES
- Vice President of Manufacturing, American Society of Mechanical Engineers (ASME) — elected Honorary Member of ASME
- President, ASM International (ASMI) — making her and Stephen the only married couple to both serve as ASMI president
- Member, Executive Committee of the Committee of Science and the Arts, Franklin Institute (Philadelphia)

## Biographical Narrative

Judy enters the appendix through Stephen Michael Copley's and Amy Copley Geist's sketches as both a family figure and a major academic leader. Born in Wakefield, Yorkshire, she earned a PhD from Cambridge and completed postdoctoral research at Imperial College London, Stony Brook, and UC Berkeley before joining the USC Materials Science Department, where she met Stephen.

After marrying Stephen in 1984, Judy's career continued alongside his. They moved to the Illinois Institute of Technology in 1990, where Amy was born and where Judy advanced in materials engineering. In 2002 she accepted the headship of Penn State's Engineering Science and Mechanics Department, prompting the family's move to State College.

The appendix emphasizes Judy's professional leadership in engineering societies and her shared professional world with Stephen. Amy's sketch adds the family dimension: travel to engineering meetings, a globally connected community of engineers, French au pairs in Hinsdale, and the academic environment that shaped Amy's later international interests.
