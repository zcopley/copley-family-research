RQ-M1 INTERPRETATION

Does direct image review strengthen or weaken the Irish-cluster interpretation?

It strongly strengthens it. Across both census years and all districts examined, the images confirm a persistent Ireland-born rural cluster in Lewis County. In 1860 (Lewis County, VA) the Dolan, Murry, Loftus, and McMahan households are all confirmed Ireland-born, ranging from recent immigrant laborer boarders (James Mc Mahan, 28; the Weston boarding-house men) to established farming families with multiple decades of residence (Thomas Dolan, 50, with Ireland-born teenage children Patrick 15 and John 13, indicating the family emigrated in the mid-to-late 1840s, consistent with Famine-era migration). In 1870 (Lewis County, WV) the Battelle cluster -- Bush (52), Leely (46), Mulooly (53) -- are all Ireland-born farming household heads with $1,200-$1,800 real estate values, indicating economic establishment after roughly two decades in the county. The Willey cluster (Hanrahan, Cummings, Casey) shows continued Ireland-born presence into the post-Civil War period.

Does it change the Battelle vs. Willey comparison?

The evidence confirms Battelle as the stronger 1870 cluster by property value and household density. The Battelle households (Bush, Leely, Mulooly at $1,200-$1,800 real estate) are substantially more propertied than the Willey households (Cummings at about $100-$200; Hanrahan unreadable due to image quality). The Mary Casey household (Willey, page 390) is a single-parent household with nine children and no real estate value visible in the index -- suggesting lower economic standing. Battelle should be the priority district for any subsequent work seeking established Irish-American farming families.

Does it change any Murray-specific conclusion?

The direct image reading of image 120 / page 114 confirms Luke Murry (38, Ireland, $600/$400, Farmer) as a real and economically modest farming household. Ann Murry (24, Ireland) is substantially younger than Luke -- possibly a second wife or a young first wife, consistent with a man who may have emigrated earlier and married later. The 4-year-old Patrick and 2-year-old John are American-born, placing the family's arrival before 1856 at minimum. The presence of a second adult Patrick Murry (34, Ireland) in the same household -- the person the user called the "Luke and Patrick Murry household" -- is confirmed as a separate adult male, not Luke's son. This Patrick (34, Ireland) is either a brother or a kin-boarder. Neither Luke nor Patrick Murry appears on the 1870 West Virginia census target pages, which is consistent with the prior note that the 1870 Lewis County clustering centers on Battelle and Willey Irish families rather than a Murray presence. The 1860 Murry cluster (Luke + adult Patrick) does not have a clear 1870 successor household in the images examined -- a gap that the prior workflow identified as a research open question and that this session does not resolve.
