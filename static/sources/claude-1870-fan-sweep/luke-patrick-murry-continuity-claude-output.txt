RQ-M1 INTERPRETATION

Did Luke remain in Lewis County, move elsewhere, die before 1870, or disappear from indexed records?

Luke Murry left Lewis County between late 1866 and early 1867 and relocated to North Providence / Providence, Rhode Island. The FamilySearch tree timeline pinpoints the transition: his last child born in West Virginia was Annie (about 1866), and his next child, Margaret Ellen, was born 13 March 1867 in North Providence RI. He is indexed and confirmed in Providence for the 1870, 1880, and 1899 (death) record events. He died in Providence on 1 November 1899, aged approximately 77, as a Day Laborer. He does not appear to have returned to Lewis County. His emigration from Lewis County to RI was likely driven by post-Civil War economic conditions and the proximity of Providence's textile and labor economy.

The reason the 1870 Lewis County / West Virginia sweep found no Ireland-born Murray household was that Luke had already left the county by early 1867 -- more than three years before the 1870 enumeration.

Did adult Patrick remain near Luke or separate?

Adult Patrick separated from Luke geographically. He stayed in Lewis County WV, married there in December 1862, raised a family there, and died there on 22 August 1871. He never followed Luke to Rhode Island. The two men diverged: Luke moved northeast to an urban/industrial economy; Patrick remained a Lewis County farmer until his death.

Does either man connect to Battelle, Willey, Cove Lick, Camden, Loveberry, St. Michael's, or the Copley/Dolan cluster?

No direct connection found for either man. Luke's 1870-1880 Rhode Island records show no references to any of those place names or surnames. Patrick's Lewis County death record (Sand Fork of Kent) places him in a different Lewis County locality than Cove Lick/Battelle/Camden. No Copley, Dolan, or Battelle surname appears in either household in any census or vital record examined. Evidence is absent rather than negative -- these collections were not exhaustively searched for those surnames, only the Murry/Murray continuity records were examined.

Does this strengthen, weaken, or leave unchanged the Murray-anchor hypothesis?

This session does not address the Murray-anchor hypothesis directly (that task was explicitly out of scope). However, two structural findings are relevant for downstream consideration: (1) Anne Loghan's surname is now confirmed -- the wife of Luke Murry was Anne Loghan/Logan, and Owen Logan in the 1860 household was almost certainly her brother; (2) the adult Patrick's 1871 death record names his father as Luke Murry -- matching Luke Murry's own father's name per the FamilySearch tree, which is consistent with a sibling relationship between the 1860 household head and the 1860 co-resident adult Patrick. Whether those Loghan/Logan and Murry family connections produce any additional Murray-anchor link to the Battelle cluster, Cove Lick, or the Copley/Dolan group has not been tested in this session.
