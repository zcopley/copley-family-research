6. DOES DISTRICT 30 (1850) MAP TO BATTELLE TOWNSHIP (1870)?

Evidence suggesting yes, partial but probable:

1. Both use Weston as the post office center. The 1860 sub-district explicitly uses Weston P.O. The 1870 Battelle header also shows Weston. District 30 in 1850 would have been served by Weston too.

2. Continuity of Irish farming households. The 1850 Coopley households are Ireland-born farmers in District 30. The 1870 Battelle Irish households are Ireland-born farmers. This is consistent with the same rural farming area northwest / west of Weston.

3. The 1860 blank sub-district was enumerated by Alfred H. [?], a different marshal from 1850 Chas. James. This fits the known fact that Virginia renumbered magisterial districts between 1850 and 1860.

4. White's 1875 map shows Lewis County divided into five districts. Courthouse district contains Weston and is the central zone. District 30 of 1850 was likely the predecessor of this Courthouse / hinterland area, which then became Battelle Township under 1870 West Virginia township reorganization.

Cautions:
- The 1860 sub-district is explicitly blank. The census enumerator did not write a name.
- The 1875 White map labels districts differently from the 1870 census. Battelle Township does not appear on the White map.
- No direct documentary evidence links "District No. 30" to "Battelle Township" by name.

Provisional conclusion: 1850 District 30 = rural area southwest-to-west of Weston, served by the Weston post office. This almost certainly includes or overlaps with what became Battelle Township in 1870. The Coopley households in 1850 and the Mulooly / Bush / Leely / McDonald households in 1870 are likely in the same general farming community, both centered on Weston and both populated by Ireland-born farmers.

7. WHAT REMAINS UNRESOLVED

1. Exact boundaries of Battelle Township vs. 1850 District 30 cannot be confirmed without a Lewis County township plat or county history that maps township boundaries. White's 1875 map is too coarse-grained to identify Battelle Township by name.

2. The 1860 sub-district name is permanently blank from the census. A state-level source, West Virginia Archives, Lewis County historical atlas, or Hacker's Creek Pioneer Descendants publication would be needed.

3. The Willey district geographic extent is unknown from census headers alone. Willey appears late in the film, suggesting a large district that may cover a different part of Lewis County from Battelle.

4. Mary Casey, born 1830 in Ireland, Willey, HH 8, Page 390, was encountered during the Willey header check and was not in the prior Ireland-born inventory. She should be added to the next session's flagged list.

5. The 1860 marshal's name, Alfred H. [Jostrom/Jarrum?], is partially illegible from the thumbnail. Direct image reading of images 119-120 with full zoom could recover this name.

6. The Battelle marshal name, [Folley?], is only partially readable from the thumbnail. Recovering it would help confirm the enumeration officer roster.

9. INTERPRETATION

The result strongly supports a Weston / courthouse-hinterland interpretation. All three census years, 1850 District 30, 1860 blank sub-district, and 1870 Battelle Township, point to Weston as the postal center. The Ireland-born farming households cluster within easy range of Weston. The 1870 Battelle Township is almost certainly part of the Weston hinterland, the farming territory surrounding the county seat.

The evidence does not support Cove Lick, Camden, or Loveberry as the primary location for the Battelle Irish cluster. Those place names do not appear in the post office or index data for the Ireland-born households reviewed.

Willey is a separate geographic zone. The Hanrahan, Cummings, and Casey households in Willey also use Weston as a post office, but their enumeration date, June 12, differs from Battelle, August 22, suggesting Willey may be a more rural or distant district enumerated earlier in summer 1870.
