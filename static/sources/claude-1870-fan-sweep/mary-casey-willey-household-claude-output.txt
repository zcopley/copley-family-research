RQ-M1 INTERPRETATION

Mary Casey is the probable wife of Thomas Casey, head age 47, born Ireland, in a nuclear family household in Willey, page 390. The household has three members: a middle-aged Irish-born couple and a Virginia-born teenage son, John Casey, age 15. This is consistent with an immigrant Irish family that arrived in Virginia by about 1855 and remained in Lewis County through 1870.

Mary Casey is not a boarder, not institutional, and not isolated as a lone person. She is part of an Ireland-born married-couple household. On image 209 itself, the Caseys are the only confirmed Ireland-born household. The Byrne household has an Irish surname but unverified birthplace. The adjacent Carey household on image 210 may represent another Irish-origin household and deserves follow-up.

Geographic position: Page 390 is near the end of the Willey enumeration. This suggests the Caseys lived in an outer portion of Willey Township, likely a mixed Irish / American-born farming neighborhood rather than a dense Irish enclave like Battelle image 8.
