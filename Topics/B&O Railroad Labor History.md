# B&O Railroad Labor History

## Historical Context
In the 1840s-1850s, the Baltimore & Ohio Railroad expansion relied heavily on immigrant labor, especially Irish workers. Conditions were typically harsh: dangerous grading/tunneling work, unstable camp conditions, and irregular pay structures through contractor systems.

This labor history is central to understanding how many newly arrived Irish families moved from port entry into inland settlement.
