# Irish Immigration to West Virginia

## Historical Context
Irish migration into what became West Virginia came in overlapping waves tied to transportation corridors, labor demand, and affordable land opportunities. Workers often entered through Atlantic ports, moved through rail/turnpike labor systems, and then transitioned into family farming and local trade.

This pattern provides the macro-framework for Copley migration and settlement in Lewis County.

## Timeline
- **1830s**: Copley-linked arrivals documented/reported in U.S. passenger lists.
- **1840s**: Irish labor flows into transportation corridors in Mid-Atlantic/Appalachian zones.
- **1843**: Copley/Hoffman land agreement marks permanent settlement arc.
- **Late 1800s**: Consolidation of family land and local social institution ties.
- **1900 onward**: Oil-era inflection and later upward educational mobility.
