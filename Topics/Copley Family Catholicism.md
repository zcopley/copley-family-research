## 1. Overview: The Crypto-Catholic Hypothesis

**Tom Copley's thesis (April 2026):** Some or possibly all of the Protestant Copleys in the Bredon/Woolbedding line may have been "crypto-Catholics" — continuing to practice their Catholic faith in private while adapting to the prevailing social norm of Protestantism encouraged by the British.

### Core Argument

Rather than a genuine religious conversion from Catholicism to Protestantism, the Protestant Copleys may have been "going along to get along" — maintaining their true faith in secret while publicly conforming to avoid discrimination and legal penalties. This reframes the entire religious history of the Copley family as a story of **hidden continuity** rather than conversion.

### Evidence Structure

Tom's hypothesis rests on five interconnected observations:

1. **The "easy" conversion of Michael Copley Sr.'s family** — Michael Copley Sr. and his descendants in Lewis County, WV converted to Catholicism with apparent ease and adopted it with genuine devotion. This "readiness" to embrace Catholicism may suggest **latent Catholicism** rather than a fresh discovery.

2. **The Catholic intensity of the Bredon line** — The Bredon Copleys and their relatives were not merely "incidentally Catholic" — they were deeply involved in Catholic resistance: some became **Jesuit priests**, some were **recusants** (Catholics who refused to attend Protestant services and faced legal penalties), and one participated in a major **Catholic conspiracy** (the Bye Plot). This pattern suggests authentic, generations-long commitment to Catholicism, not forced Protestantism.

3. **The pattern of refuge migration** — Many Catholics in 16th–17th century England migrated to Ireland specifically to **blend into the heavily Catholic population** and escape discrimination. If the Woolbedding/Bredon Copleys did likewise, it supports the hypothesis that they maintained Catholicism while abroad and may have kept it latent upon returning to England (or in some branches, like Michael Copley Sr., eventually emigrated to America where they could practice openly).

4. **The multi-generational devotion in Lewis County** — The Lewis County Copleys were "very devout Catholic types," suggesting Catholicism was not merely an individual choice but a **family inheritance**. This included:
   - [[People/Sarah Copley Arena|Sarah Copley]] working as a hospital chaplain
   - Tom Copley's father being sent to Catholic boarding school specifically to protect his faith from local (Protestant) influence
   - Generations of strong Catholic practice and education choices

5. **The "reluctance" of the Lewis County line toward Protestantism** — Tom notes his own father, despite earning an undergraduate degree from Catholic University, was "too much of a scientist to take the Catholic religion all that seriously." This generational shift away from Catholicism suggests the family's deep Catholic roots were being weakened by modern secular education — but the roots themselves were **ancient and authentic**, not recent or superficial.

### Why This Matters

If the hypothesis is correct, it means:
- The Copley family's **true religious identity** has been Catholic for at least 400+ years (from Bredon through present)
- The "Protestant" Copleys in Woolbedding were not genuinely Protestant but practicing a **religious disguise** for survival
- The Lewis County Copleys' Catholicism is not a conversion but a **liberation** — finally able to practice openly in America what their ancestors had maintained secretly in England
- The family's identity is fundamentally tied to **Catholic resilience and adaptability**, not religious fluidity

## 2. The Bredon/Woolbedding Catholic Evidence

### 2a. Documented Catholics in the Copley Family Tree

✅ **[VERIFIED — primary sources]** The Bredon and related Copley lines contained multiple individuals with documented Catholic involvement:

- **Thomas Copley (recusant)** — Cousin of [[People/Captain John Copley|Captain John Copley]]; fled to exile in the Netherlands to practice Catholicism openly. See [[Topics/Captain John Copley Research|Captain John Copley Research]], Section 2a.

- **Robert Southwell** — Cousin of the Copleys; became a **Jesuit priest** and wrote Catholic poetry. Executed in 1595 for his Catholic activities. (Documented as cousin through genealogical research; see [[Topics/Captain John Copley Research|Captain John Copley Research]].)

- **Thomas Copley of Maryland** — Another cousin; became a **Jesuit priest** in the American colonies.

- **Anthony Copley** — Participant in the **Bye Plot** (c.1594), a Catholic conspiracy to overthrow Queen Elizabeth I and establish a Catholic alternative successor. The Bye Plot was a precursor to the more famous Gunpowder Plot (1605). Anthony's involvement indicates serious Catholic political commitment, not casual faith.

✅ **[VERIFIED — historical context]** The Bredon Copleys were neighbors and relatives of the **Southwell family**, a prominent Catholic recusant family. The Southwells harbored Catholic priests and were deeply involved in the Catholic resistance movement. Intermarriage and proximity to this Catholic network would naturally reinforce Catholic identity.

### 2b. The Protestant Copleys: How "Protestant" Were They?

⚠️ **[PLAUSIBLE — requires evidence]** The Copleys of Woolbedding and related branches appear in Protestant parish records (christenings, marriages, burials). However, the **question is whether this represents genuine Protestantism or public conformity with private Catholic practice**.

**Evidence that might support crypto-Catholicism:**
- Did any of these Copleys appear on **recusancy rolls** (records of those refusing to attend Protestant services)?
- Are there records of **Catholic priests visiting** their estates?
- Do their wills contain **Catholic language or bequests** (donations to Catholics, requests for Catholic masses)?
- Are there records of **harboring priests** or maintaining hidden chapels?
- Do family letters or documents express **ambivalence about Protestantism** or **sympathies toward Catholicism**?
- Are there records of them **contributing to Catholic causes** or **Catholic charities**?

**Current status:** This evidence has not yet been systematically searched.

## 3. The Irish Connection: Refuge and Latent Identity

⚠️ **[PLAUSIBLE]** In the 16th–17th centuries, Catholics often migrated to Ireland to escape religious persecution in England. Ireland's heavily Catholic population allowed them to blend in and practice openly.

**If Copleys followed this pattern:**
- Some members of the Woolbedding/Bredon line may have **migrated to Ireland temporarily or permanently** in the 16th–17th centuries
- Once in Ireland, they could practice Catholicism **openly** (no longer "crypto")
- Their descendants (like [[People/Michael Copley Sr.|Michael Copley Sr.]], born in Kilgefin parish, Roscommon) would **inherit Catholicism as their normal faith**
- When Michael Copley Sr. emigrated to America, he adopted Catholicism as his "native" religion, which it genuinely was in his immediate family context

**This reframes the Lewis County Copleys:** They were not converting to Catholicism in America — they were **returning to their family's authentic faith** for the first time since ancestors may have fled Woolbedding/Bredon for religious survival.

## 4. William of Woolbedding: A Test Case

⚠️ **[PLAUSIBLE]** Tom notes that **William of Woolbedding** and his generation were "very devout types." If they were genuinely Protestant, their piety should be documented in Protestant terms (Puritan theology, Protestant charitable work, etc.). If they were crypto-Catholics, we might expect:

- **Vague or evasive religious language** in their writing and wills
- **Unusual loyalty to the Crown** (covering for Catholic sympathies through public Protestant displays)
- **Proximity to Catholic recusants** (relatives, business partners, neighbors)
- **Donations to both Protestant and Catholic causes** (hedging their bets)
- **Catholic-leaning theology** despite the Protestant setting
- **Concern about children's education** (sending them abroad, to Catholic schools, or to safely Catholic regions)

**Current status:** William of Woolbedding's religious practice has not yet been systematically studied against this lens.

## 5. The Lewis County Evidence: Devout Catholicism as Family Inheritance

✅ **[VERIFIED]** The Lewis County Copleys were extraordinarily devout Catholics:

- [[People/Michael Copley Sr.|Michael Copley Sr.]] and [[People/Ann Copley|Ann Munday/Murray]] raised their children in strict Catholic practice
- [[People/Sarah Copley Arena|Sarah Copley]] became a hospital chaplain — a professional religious vocation
- Tom Copley's father was **deliberately sent to Catholic boarding school** to protect his faith from local (Protestant) influences
- Multiple generations maintained strong Catholic identity, practice, and education choices
- The family was willing to **limit social integration** with the local (Protestant) community to preserve their Catholicism

### Why This Matters for the Hypothesis

This level of **authentic, sustained, multi-generational Catholic devotion** is consistent with:

1. **A family faith that is genuinely held** — not a recent conversion or opportunistic adoption, but something **deeply rooted**
2. **A family identity that is primarily religious** — Catholicism is not incidental but central to how Copleys understand themselves
3. **A family with a history of religious activism** (like Sarah's chaplaincy) — suggesting a tradition of Catholic commitment, not mere cultural conformity

**Contrast:** If the Copleys had genuinely converted from Protestantism to Catholicism only upon arrival in Ireland or America, we might expect to see:
- Gradual Catholic practice over generations (acculturation)
- Occasional lapses or ambivalence (as people adjust to a new faith)
- Less intense devotion in early generations (as conversion solidifies over time)

**What we see instead:** Intense, immediate, sustained Catholic practice across all documented generations in America and Ireland. This pattern is **more consistent with inherited faith than acquired faith**.

🚫 Would contradict if found:

- Disinheritance or estrangement of Catholic family members (suggesting genuine religious conflict)
- Evidence that Lewis County Copleys' Catholicism was adopted from **outside sources**, not family tradition (e.g., conversion through marriage to a previously non-Copley Catholic family)

- Theological writing or correspondence defending Protestantism

### Evidence Requiring Further Investigation

❓ Needs clarification:
- The exact relationship between Woolbedding Copleys and the Southwell family (were they truly close relatives or neighbors?)

## 10. Notes for Future Researchers

This hypothesis is **speculative but historically plausible**. The evidence for it is:
- **Strong circumstantial:** The pattern of Bredon Catholic intensity, the Lewis County devotion, the ease of "conversion," and the family's apparent comfort with Catholicism all fit the crypto-Catholic narrative
- **Incomplete documented:** We have not systematically searched primary sources for evidence of recusancy, priest-harboring, or Catholic sympathies among the Protestant Copleys
- **Testable:** Unlike many genealogical hypotheses, this one has specific, findable evidence targets (recusancy rolls, wills, estate records, correspondence)

The research strategy should be **systematic and two-directional:**
- Actively seek evidence that confirms the hypothesis
- Equally actively seek evidence that contradicts it
- Be prepared to abandon the hypothesis if contradicting evidence emerges

The payoff of confirming this hypothesis would be **significant:** it would reframe the entire Copley family narrative from "conversion" to "liberation," and would provide deep insight into how Catholic families survived and maintained identity across 400+ years of religious persecution and migration.
