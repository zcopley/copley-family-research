Major Discovery: Mary Copely Giblin — Iowa Connection (NEW April 22, 2026)

The Finding

Mary Copely Giblin (1814–1884):

  • Born: 1814, Tully townland, Kilcorkey Civil Parish, County Roscommon, Ireland
  • Death: 27 October 1884, age 70
  • Burial: Saint Ann’s Cemetery, Vail, Crawford County, Iowa, United States
  • Source: Find a Grave Memorial #8468680

Impact on Family History

  1. Establishes Iowa settlement branch — First confirmed Copely emigrant from Roscommon cluster (besides Michael Sr.) to America
  2. Broader diaspora pattern — Shows the Roscommon Copleys dispersed across multiple American states (WV, Iowa, others TBD)
  3. Personal significance to Zach: Zach Copley (G26) relocated to Iowa in 2025 with his family; his wife’s family is from Iowa. An ancestral line is now documented in the same state where Zach currently lives — a remarkable intersection of family history and personal geography.

2. Geographic Relationships Clarified and Connected

Fairymount as Suburb of Castlerea

NEW INSIGHT: Fairymount is essentially a suburb of Castlerea, not a distant location. This strengthens the connection between:

  • Catholic Kilgefin/Fairymount Copleys (William and Michael Copely, farmers, 1794–1864)
  • Protestant Castlerea/Termon Beg branch (Griffith’s Valuation Copleys, 1857)

Tom’s assessment: “It’s too much of a coincidence—these families (Castlerea and Kilgefin/Fairymount) must be related somehow.”

Distance Measurements

  • Kilgefin to Termon Beg: Approximately 10 km apart (not distant)
  • Ballintober to Castlerea: Approximately 9 miles in the opposite direction from Fairymount
  • Geographic scale analogy: “It’s about like the distance of the center of Berkeley to the center of El Cerrito, and Fairymount is about 9 miles in the opposite direction from Castlerea. That’s like Berkeley to Oakland. These places are all very close together there in Ireland.”

Impact: This scale analogy fundamentally changes how we evaluate Explanation B (Termon Beg origin with Catholic conversion). These are neighboring communities where families would naturally interact and intermarry, not distant locations that happened to share a surname.

Updated location: Section 13 (The Catholic Copely Branch) now includes this geographic context with scale analogy.
