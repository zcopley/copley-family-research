# RQ-M1 Lewis County FAN Sweep

This page is the findings log for census and neighbor-network research around the Murray Settlement hypothesis in Lewis County, Virginia / West Virginia.

**Current status:** The bounded Claude for Chrome FAN-sweep passes for **1850**, **1860**, and **1870** are now complete, and the key 1860 / 1870 cluster pages now have a direct image-capture pass plus a Murry continuity pass. The 1850 pass found the **Micheal / Patrick Coopley** household in **District 30**, but no broader Irish surname cluster. The 1860 image capture confirms a dense Ireland-born cluster centered on **Dolan / Murry / Tierney** rural farming households, plus a separate **Weston** boarding-house / labor cluster around **Loftus / Barret / Mc Mahan** and other Ireland-born workers. The Murry continuity search shows **Luke Murry** moved from Lewis County to Providence, Rhode Island between late 1866 and March 1867, while the separate adult **Patrick Murry** stayed in Lewis County, appeared in Sheridan / Lewis in 1870, and died at Sand Fork of Kent in 1871. The 1870 image work confirms rural Irish farming clusters in **Battelle** and **Willey**, with **Battelle image 8** now the strongest propertied cluster: **Bush / Leely / Mulooly / McDonald**. The district-geography pass still points the 1850-1870 rural cluster evidence toward the **Weston / courthouse hinterland** rather than directly toward Cove Lick / Camden / Loveberry. The next bounded Murray-specific work is to capture the underlying Murry marriage / death images and map Sheridan / Sand Fork of Kent against the settlement geography.

## Research Question

Did Murray, Copley, Dolan, Hanley, Hannon, Gillooly, Reynolds, Mullooly, Mulroney, Mahon, or related Irish Catholic households cluster together in Lewis County before or near the 1843 Copley land purchase?

This matters because a census-neighbor cluster near Cove Lick, Camden, Loveberry, St. Michael's, or the Copley parcel would support the idea that the settlement was a coordinated Irish Catholic community rather than isolated family migration.

**Map check:** M. Wood White's 1875 county and district map shows Lewis County magisterial districts including **Courthouse**, **Hackers Creek**, **Freemans Creek**, **Collins Settlement**, and **Skidmore**, but it does not label `Battelle Township` or `Willey` under those names. The map supports a central Weston / Courthouse-Hackers Creek orientation but does not prove exact 1870 township boundaries.

**Working interpretation:** The 1850-1870 rural Irish farming evidence currently points most directly to the **Weston / courthouse hinterland**. It does not yet directly place the Battelle cluster in Cove Lick, Camden, or Loveberry. The relationship between those settlement place names, the 1843 Copley parcel, and the census district labels remains a map / land-record problem.

## Evaluation Rules

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Murray household appears in Lewis County by 1840, near Copley / Camden / Loveberry / Catholic settlement surnames | Strong support for Murray anchor-family hypothesis. |
| Murray household appears by 1850 near Copley and settlement-family households | Moderate support; compare with deed evidence for earlier arrival. |
| Murray household appears in Lewis County but far from the Copley settlement zone | Partial support; requires mapping before changing conclusions. |
| No Murray household appears, but Murray deed evidence is confirmed | Census gap or temporary absence; deed evidence remains primary. |
| Murray households appear, but are entirely Virginia-born and not near the Irish-origin Copley household | Weak support for a Murray-centered Irish settlement in that census year; may indicate a separate Virginia Murray line. |
| No Murray household or deed evidence appears after variant searching | Weakens the anchor-family hypothesis; settlement name origin remains unresolved. |
| Munday / Monday / Mundy household appears near Copleys before 1850 | Reopens the Ann Munday surname question and requires direct-record review. |
