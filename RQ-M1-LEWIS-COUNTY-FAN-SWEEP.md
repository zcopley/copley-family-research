# RQ-M1 Lewis County FAN Sweep

This page is the findings log for census and neighbor-network research around the Murray Settlement hypothesis in Lewis County, Virginia / West Virginia.

**Current status:** Ready for incoming Ancestry and FamilySearch census findings. The prior FamilySearch search found no independent Munday / Monday households in Lewis County, 1840-1860; the broader Murray / Copley / settlement-family FAN sweep is still pending.

## Research Question

Did Murray, Copley, Dolan, Hanley, Hannon, Gillooly, Reynolds, Mullooly, Mulroney, Mahon, or related Irish Catholic households cluster together in Lewis County before or near the 1843 Copley land purchase?

This matters because a census-neighbor cluster near Cove Lick, Camden, Loveberry, St. Michael's, or the Copley parcel would support the idea that the settlement was a coordinated Irish Catholic community rather than isolated family migration.

## Evaluation Rules

| Finding | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| Murray household appears in Lewis County by 1840, near Copley / Camden / Loveberry / Catholic settlement surnames | Strong support for Murray anchor-family hypothesis. |
| Murray household appears by 1850 near Copley and settlement-family households | Moderate support; compare with deed evidence for earlier arrival. |
| Murray household appears in Lewis County but far from the Copley settlement zone | Partial support; requires mapping before changing conclusions. |
| No Murray household appears, but Murray deed evidence is confirmed | Census gap or temporary absence; deed evidence remains primary. |
| No Murray household or deed evidence appears after variant searching | Weakens the anchor-family hypothesis; settlement name origin remains unresolved. |
| Munday / Monday / Mundy household appears near Copleys before 1850 | Reopens the Ann Munday surname question and requires direct-record review. |
