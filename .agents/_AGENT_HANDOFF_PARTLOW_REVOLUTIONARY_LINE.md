## Current Evidence Assessment

- **Benjamin Partlow service:** strong. Pension cover sheet plus pension-roll lead support Revolutionary War service. Still need full NARA/Fold3 pension file.
- **Frank Partlow -> Marion M. Partlow:** now census-supported online through New England Ball Project citations to the 1880 and 1900 Johnson Township census.
- **Marion M. Partlow -> John H. Partlow:** census-supported online. New England Ball Project cites 1850 and 1860 Johnson Township census entries naming Marion in John H.'s household.
- **John H. Partlow -> Jacob Partlow:** family manuscript plus derivative online lead. Needs original proof.
- **Jacob Partlow -> Benjamin Partlow:** family manuscript plus Ancestry screenshot. This is the critical proof gap.
- Broad public web searching did not resolve the Jacob -> Benjamin link; move next to compiled Partlow books, Benjamin probate/estate, and Culpeper/Clark land/tax records.
- **Frank Partlow = Nollie Franklin Partlow:** likely, but not yet proved. Resolve first with marriage/census/death/obituary records.
- **Alice Rude = Mary Alice Rude/Partlow:** likely, based on the 1977 manuscript and Ancestry screenshot. Needs original proof.
- **Immediate record target:** use the Illinois Statewide Marriage Index in a browser, or request the record from Clark County Clerk/Recorder, for the reported 10 Jun 1900 Frank/Nollie Partlow and Alice/Mary Alice Rude marriage. Search variants: Frank Partlow, Nollie Franklin Partlow, N. F. Partlow, Alice Rude, Mary Alice Rude.

## Important Caveats

- Treat the handwritten lineage as a strong family-source lead, not proof.
- The typed 1960 Harry C. Partlow letter discusses a Partlow, Virginia branch around Captain L. / Lancelot Partlow. That may be collateral and should not be merged into Zach's direct line without proof.
